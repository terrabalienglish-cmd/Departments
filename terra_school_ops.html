School
Operations

Terra School is the structured learning layer of the Terra ecosystem. Where workshops offer inspiration and mentorship offers guidance, School offers knowledge — sequenced, practical, and designed to build real capability in founders. This manual defines how courses are designed, delivered, filled, and improved. Nothing left to improvisation.

Context

Where School Sits in the Ecosystem

Terra School is the middle of the founder journey. Not the entry point — that's workshops. Not the deepest layer — that's mentorship. School is where curiosity becomes capability. It's the bridge that takes someone who showed up to a free workshop and turns them into someone ready for the intensity of the mentorship program.

Entry
Workshops
Free. Open. Introduction to Terra and to ideas. No commitment required.
School — this department
Structured Learning
Paid. Enrolled. Knowledge that builds over time. Commitment required.
Deep Work
Mentorship Program
Selective. Intense. Personalised growth over 8 weeks with a mentor.
Community
Alumni Network
Terra graduates. Ongoing connection, events, and opportunity.
School's Dual Purpose
Terra School serves two goals simultaneously. The obvious one: it gives founders structured, practical knowledge they can apply immediately. The strategic one: it is the highest-quality pipeline source for the mentorship program. Founders who complete a School course are more committed, better prepared, and clearer on what they want from mentorship than cold applicants. The School Director must always hold both goals at once.
Section 01

Course Architecture

Three course tiers. Each has a fixed structure, a fixed length, and a fixed maximum class size. The tier determines depth. The topic changes each run. The structure does not.

Tier 01 · Foundation
Foundation Course
2 weeks · 4 sessions · Max 15 students
For founders who are early-stage or exploring a specific topic for the first time. Dense, practical, and outcome-focused. By the end, a student should be able to do one specific thing they couldn't do before. Not inspired — capable.
Example topics: Writing to convert. Building your first sales system. Understanding your numbers. Hiring your first team member.
Tier 02 · Intensive
Intensive Course
4 weeks · 8 sessions · Max 12 students
For founders with some traction who want to go deeper on a domain. Combines teaching sessions with practical application and peer review. Students leave with both knowledge and documented work they've produced during the course.
Example topics: Growth strategy and distribution. Building and leading a small team. Brand positioning for a founder business. Revenue model design.
Tier 03 · Masterclass
Masterclass
6 weeks · 12 sessions · Max 8 students
For experienced founders working on a complex, specific challenge. Led by one or more expert instructors. Small cohort. Deep peer relationships. Students leave having solved or significantly progressed a real business problem they brought in on Day 1.
Example topics: Scaling operations beyond the founder. Fundraising readiness. International expansion. Building a business that runs without you.
The Invariable Rule on Class Size
Class sizes are not targets — they are hard ceilings. A Foundation course that attracts 20 applicants does not expand to 20 students. It runs at 15 and opens a waitlist for the next run. The quality of a Terra School course is directly dependent on the ratio of instructor attention to student. The moment that ratio breaks, the quality breaks with it. If demand exceeds capacity consistently, the solution is more course runs — not bigger classes.
Section 02

Curriculum Design Process

Every course starts as an idea. The curriculum design process turns that idea into a live, quality-approved learning experience. No course goes live without completing every step in this process.

Process — From Idea to Live Curriculum
01
Identify course need from community signal
Course ideas come from: workshop Q&A patterns (what founders keep asking), mentorship program themes (what mentees struggle with most), School Director's own observation of the community's gaps, direct founder feedback. Every course idea is logged in Notion with the source signal. Ideas without a clear community need do not move forward.
Trigger: Ongoing. Reviewed quarterly during planning.
School DirectorNotion idea log
02
Define the course brief
Before designing any content, write the brief. One page in Notion. Covers: the one outcome a student will achieve by completion, who this is for (ICP and stage), why now, which tier it belongs in, suggested instructor profile, and what students will produce or be able to do. If you can't write this clearly, the course isn't ready to be designed yet.
Trigger: Course idea approved for development
School DirectorNotion
03
Identify and approach instructor
The instructor must have done the thing they are teaching — not studied it. Use Terra's mentor pool, workshop speaker alumni, and the School Director's network. Brief them on Terra's teaching philosophy: practical, specific, story-led. No academic delivery. No death-by-slides. If an instructor won't adapt their style to Terra's standard, they are not the right instructor regardless of their credentials.
Trigger: Brief approved
School DirectorDirect conversation
04
Co-design the session-by-session curriculum
School Director and instructor design the curriculum together using the Curriculum Template in Notion. Every session has: a learning objective (one sentence — what students can do after this session), the core content, a practical exercise, and a homework task. School Director reviews for consistency, pacing, and outcome alignment. Instructor does not design alone — the curriculum is a collaboration.
Trigger: Instructor confirmed
School Director + InstructorNotion curriculum template
05
COO review and sign-off
Every new course is reviewed by COO before launch. COO checks: Does the outcome match a genuine community need? Is the pricing correct for the tier? Is the instructor credible? Is this the right time for this course? COO signs off in Notion. Without sign-off, the course does not launch.
Trigger: Curriculum complete and ready for review
COO approvesNotion sign-off field
06
Brief Marketing on course launch campaign
Written brief with: course name, tier, duration, price, outcome statement, ICP description, launch date, enrolment deadline, the story angle, and any instructor profile content. Marketing creates the campaign. School Director approves all assets before publishing — same 24h turnaround rule as Workshops.
Trigger: COO sign-off received. Minimum 4 weeks before launch.
School Director → MarketingNotion brief
07
Open enrolment
Enrolment form live. School Director monitors applications daily. If enrolment is not hitting pace by Week 2 of campaign, escalate to School Director + Marketing for a review — not at the deadline when nothing can be done.
Trigger: Launch date. Applications open for 3 weeks.
School Director + MarketingGoogle Form

Section 03

Enrolment & Student Journey

From the moment someone applies to the moment they graduate. Every step is defined, every owner is clear, and no student falls through a gap because a process didn't exist.

Process — Enrolment
01
Application received and logged
Google Form submission auto-populates the student applications sheet. School Director reviews new applications daily during the open window — not in a batch at the deadline. Early review means early follow-up for strong candidates and early management of borderline ones.
Trigger: Form submitted by applicant
Compliance (auto-log) + School Director (review)Google Sheet
02
Screen applications against course criteria
School Director scores each application using the standard rubric in Notion. For Intensives and Masterclasses, shortlisted applicants receive a 15-minute intake call. Foundation courses do not require an intake call — the application form is the filter. The intake call is not an interview — it's a fit conversation. Its purpose is to set expectations and confirm commitment, not to intimidate.
Trigger: Application window closes (Foundation) or rolling review (Intensive/Masterclass)
School DirectorNotion rubric
03
Send acceptance communications
Accepted: personal email with welcome, course dates, payment instructions, what to prepare, and the pre-course assignment (see below). Waitlisted: honest, specific, timeline for next run. Declined: always responded to, never ghosted. Every response within 48 hours of decision.
Trigger: Within 48h of decision
School DirectorGmail template
04
Collect payment and confirmation
Enrolment is not confirmed until payment is received. School Director coordinates with Finance/COO on payment tracking. Compliance logs payment status in Google Sheet. If payment is outstanding 48h before course starts, School Director follows up — not chases, follows up. If payment is not received by Day 1, the seat is released to the waitlist.
Trigger: Acceptance email sent
COO/Finance + ComplianceGoogle Sheet
05
Send pre-course assignment
Every student receives a pre-course assignment the week before the course starts. Not optional. Not homework — preparation. For an Intensive: write a 200-word description of your business and the one thing you want to solve. For a Masterclass: submit a current-state analysis of the problem you're bringing into the course. Students who complete this are 40% more engaged in Session 1.
Trigger: 7 days before Course Day 1
School DirectorGmail
06
Onboarding session (Week 0 / Day 0)
30-minute online or in-person onboarding before the course begins. Covers: how sessions work, what's expected, the commitment agreement, how to get the most out of the course, and introductions. The course WhatsApp group is activated here. Instructor is present. Sets the tone for everything that follows.
Trigger: 2–3 days before Session 1
School Director + InstructorOnline / In-person
Process — Course Delivery Cadence
01
Session reminder sent 24h before every session
WhatsApp message to course group: time, location/link, what to bring, and a one-line reminder of what this session covers. Consistent format every week. Homework reminder included if assignment is due.
Trigger: 24 hours before each session
School Director or assigned VolunteerWhatsApp
02
Instructor runs session to the curriculum
School Director does not manage the session content — the curriculum does. School Director attends every session (or assigns a deputy) to observe quality, monitor engagement, and support the instructor if needed. Attendance is taken by a volunteer in the first 10 minutes and submitted to Compliance within 2 hours of session end.
Trigger: Scheduled session time
Instructor leads · School Director observesIn-person / Online
03
Session assignment posted to WhatsApp group
Immediately after each session, the instructor or School Director posts: what was covered (one sentence), the assignment for this week (specific, actionable, linked to the session's learning objective), and the deadline. No vague tasks. Every assignment has a clear output that can be reviewed.
Trigger: Within 1 hour of session end
Instructor / School DirectorWhatsApp
04
Track assignment submissions
School Director logs assignment submissions in Notion weekly tracker. Students who haven't submitted by the day before the next session receive a direct message — private, supportive, specific. "You haven't submitted the offer statement — is there something blocking you?" Not public, not shaming, not ignored.
Trigger: Assignment deadline passes
School DirectorNotion tracker
05
Mid-course check-in with instructor
After the halfway session, School Director has a brief 1:1 with the instructor. Covers: group energy and engagement, any students to watch, whether pacing needs adjusting, what support the instructor needs for the second half. Findings logged in Notion. If there are structural changes needed, they are made — the curriculum is a living document, not a fixed script.
Trigger: Halfway point of course
School Director + InstructorNotion session notes
06
Flag at-risk students before final sessions
School Director reviews attendance and assignment completion data by Week 3 of an Intensive (or Week 5 of a Masterclass). Any student below 80% attendance or missing 2+ assignments receives a private check-in. At-risk students are supported, not written off. The completion rate metric starts here — not at graduation.
Trigger: One week before final sessions begin
School DirectorNotion + WhatsApp direct
Process — Graduation & Alumni Pathway
01
Final session — presentation day
The final session of every Intensive and Masterclass is a presentation day. Each student presents the deliverable from the final assignment (their 90-day plan, their strategy document, their business case — whatever the course produced). Peer feedback happens in the room. This session is the proof of learning. School Director facilitates, instructor supports.
Trigger: Final session of course
School Director (MC) + InstructorIn-person preferred
02
Compile graduation eligibility list
Completion criteria: attended 80%+ of sessions, submitted all core assignments. School Director reviews data with Compliance. Borderline cases reviewed individually — School Director makes the final call. No group decision, no committee. The standard is clear; the decision is singular.
Trigger: Day after final session
School Director + ComplianceGoogle Sheet + Notion
03
Issue certificates and alumni status
Graduates receive their certificate by email within 3 days of final session. Compliance tags them in the master database. They are added to the Terra School Alumni WhatsApp group. Non-completers are contacted privately and kindly — with a clear path back in if they want it (next run of the same course, or a Foundation course to build the foundation they were missing).
Trigger: Within 3 days of graduation list confirmed
School Director + ComplianceGmail + Google Drive (certificates)
04
Mentorship program invitation for eligible graduates
Within 1 week of graduation, School Director personally invites every graduate to consider the mentorship program. This is not a mass email — it is a direct, personalised message that references what they worked on in the course and why mentorship would be the right next step for them. School Director coordinates with Program Director (Mentorship) on timing and cohort availability.
Trigger: Within 7 days of graduation
School Director → Program Director (Mentorship)WhatsApp direct + Gmail
05
Post-course retrospective
School Director and instructor run a 45-minute retrospective within 2 weeks of course end. Three questions: What do we keep? What do we change? What do we add? All curriculum updates are documented in Notion before the next run. Student feedback form results are reviewed here — not ignored. The course that runs next time is better than the one that just ran.
Trigger: Within 14 days of final session
School Director + InstructorNotion retrospective template
Process — Instructor Management
01
Build and maintain instructor pool
School Director maintains a database of 8–12 qualified instructors at all times. Sources: mentor alumni, workshop speakers, Terra community, external network. Every potential instructor is assessed before being added: have they done the thing they'd teach? Do they communicate in a way that works in a classroom? Would they represent Terra's values in front of students?
Trigger: Ongoing. Reviewed quarterly.
School DirectorNotion instructor database
02
Instructor onboarding for first-time facilitators
Before leading their first Terra course, every instructor completes a 1-hour onboarding with School Director. Covers: Terra's teaching philosophy, what students expect, how to run the practical exercises, how to give feedback in the room, how to handle difficult classroom dynamics, and what to do if a session runs off-track. This is mandatory. No exceptions for "experienced" facilitators.
Trigger: First course confirmed
School DirectorDirect 1:1 session
03
Instructor compensation and agreements
Every instructor signs an instructor agreement before their course is published. Covers: delivery expectations, cancellation policy, intellectual property (curriculum co-designed with Terra is Terra's), payment terms, and conduct standards. School Director coordinates with COO on compensation model. Agreements stored in Google Drive under Instructor Profiles.
Trigger: Course confirmed, before marketing launches
COO (agreement) + School Director (relationship)Google Drive

Section 06

Operating Rules

These protect the quality and integrity of Terra School. They exist because every shortcut taken in education has a compounding cost. Change them through the retrospective — not through convenience.

Non-Negotiable Operating Rules — School Department
01No course launches without COO sign-off. The brief must be written, the curriculum must be designed, and the instructor must be confirmed before any marketing goes out. In that order.
02Class size ceilings are absolute. Foundation: 15. Intensive: 12. Masterclass: 8. Demand exceeding capacity results in a waitlist and an additional run — not an expanded class size.
03Every instructor teaches from lived experience, not theory. The School Director is responsible for assessing this before any instructor is confirmed. Credentials are context, not qualification.
04Every instructor signs an instructor agreement before their course is announced. No agreement, no course. The intellectual property clause is non-negotiable — curricula co-designed with Terra belong to Terra.
05Every student signs a commitment agreement at onboarding. No agreement, no confirmed enrolment. If they won't sign, they won't commit. Both signals matter the same way.
06Every session has one clear learning objective. If the session cannot be described in "After this session, students can do X," the session is not ready to be delivered.
07Every assignment has a clear, reviewable output. "Reflect on your business" is not an assignment. "Write a 200-word description of your primary customer and the single reason they choose you over an alternative" is an assignment.
08At-risk students are contacted before the final sessions — not at graduation. The completion rate is the School Director's responsibility, not a number that happens to them.
09Every graduate receives a personal mentorship program invitation within 7 days of course completion. This is the strategic reason School exists. It does not happen automatically — it requires a direct, personalised outreach.
10Every course runs a retrospective within 14 days of completion. Curriculum updates are documented and applied before the next run. A course that runs twice with the same problems is a systems failure.
11Nothing important about a student, instructor, or course lives in personal email, WhatsApp history, or a personal device. If it's not in Notion or Google Drive, it doesn't exist for Terra.
12The School Director trains 2 successors who can run the full enrolment-to-graduation cycle independently. The curriculum document and the School Operations Manual are the primary handover tools.
