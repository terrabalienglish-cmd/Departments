Sponsorship
Operations

Sponsorship is not fundraising. It is the art of identifying businesses whose interests genuinely align with Terra's community — and building relationships that make Terra more valuable to founders, not more commercial. Every sponsor is a partner. Every partnership has clear deliverables, clear limits, and a clear renewal conversation. This department protects Terra's integrity while funding its growth.

Section 01

Sponsorship Philosophy

The single most important question in every sponsorship conversation is: does this brand make Terra more valuable to our founders, or does it make Terra more commercial? If the answer is the latter, the conversation ends.

The Right Sponsor
The right sponsor is one whose product or service a Terra founder would genuinely want to know about — independent of the sponsorship. A co-working space, a business bank account for international founders, a legal service for company formation, a tool that solves a real founder problem. The test is simple: if a workshop attendee learned about this sponsor through Terra, would they thank Terra for the introduction — or feel like they'd been sold to? The answer to that question determines whether we proceed.
What Terra Offers Sponsors
  • Access to a curated founder community
    Not a mass audience. A specific, high-quality community of founders building real businesses. The density of the right person per event is what makes Terra valuable to sponsors — not the volume.
  • Brand visibility at events
    Signage, verbal mention, materials at registration desk or in the room. Clearly visible, not intrusive.
  • Co-branded content (Partner tier and above)
    One branded content piece per month. Approved by Marketing. Must provide genuine value to founders — not a product advertisement.
  • Venue partnership (Community tier)
    For venues that host Terra events — reduced or waived venue fees in exchange for Terra's consistent presence and brand association.
What Terra Does Not Offer — Ever
  • Attendee contact data
    No sponsor receives any attendee's name, WhatsApp, or email. This is a permanent, non-negotiable limit. Any sponsor who asks for this is declined immediately — and the request is logged as a signal about their intentions.
  • Editorial control
    No sponsor influences who speaks at Terra events, what topics are covered, or what Terra's programs teach. Content is entirely Terra's domain.
  • Speaker slots in exchange for sponsorship
    Speakers are selected on merit. A sponsor who wants a speaking slot must apply through the normal speaker pipeline. Sponsorship and speaking are separate tracks that never intersect.
  • Exclusive category rights that limit Terra's freedom
    Sponsors do not get exclusivity that prevents Terra from running programs, inviting speakers, or developing partnerships that serve founders — even if they compete with the sponsor.

On Custom Arrangements
Sponsors occasionally propose arrangements that don't fit neatly into a tier — a higher budget in exchange for something non-standard, or a package combining elements from multiple tiers. These conversations are not declined outright. They are taken to COO, who evaluates whether the custom arrangement serves Terra's community interests. If it does, a custom agreement is drafted with the same rigour as a standard tier agreement. The test is always: does this serve founders, or does it compromise the environment we've built for them?
Section 03

Pipeline & Acquisition

Sponsors are not found through cold outreach. They emerge from Terra's existing network — businesses that founders already use and trust, co-working spaces that already host founders, tools that already solve the problems Terra's community faces. The pipeline is warm by design.

Process — Sponsor Acquisition

When a candidate is identified, Sponsorship Lead applies the Right Sponsor test before doing anything else. If they pass, they are added to the pipeline in Notion with the source noted.

02
Initial conversation — understand their goals before presenting ours
Sponsorship Lead initiates contact through the warmest available route — mutual connection, existing relationship, or direct outreach. The first conversation is not a pitch. It is a discovery conversation: what are they trying to achieve with their marketing? Who are they trying to reach? What does partnership mean to them? Sponsorship Lead listens for two things: genuine alignment with the Terra community, and realistic budget expectations. If either is absent, the conversation ends gracefully — the candidate is moved to "not now" in the pipeline, not deleted.

Prepare and send the proposal — one clear recommendation
After qualification, Sponsorship Lead prepares a one-to-two-page proposal from the standard template. The proposal leads with Terra's community profile — who attends, what they're building, why the community is valuable — before presenting the tier recommendation. Sponsorship Lead recommends one specific tier based on what they learned in the qualification conversation. Presenting all three tiers and asking them to choose signals that Terra hasn't done the work. One recommendation, well-reasoned. COO is informed before the proposal is sent.

If still no response, the prospect is moved to "dormant" in the pipeline — not deleted, not chased again. The right sponsors respond. Chasing the wrong ones wastes relationship capital.

Section 04

Sponsorship Fulfilment

Fulfilment is where most sponsorship relationships succeed or fail. The agreement is easy to sign. Delivering on every benefit, on time, without being chased — that is what earns renewal. The Sponsorship Lead owns this entirely. Departments execute. Sponsorship Lead tracks.

The Fulfilment Standard
Every sponsor benefit is delivered proactively — before the sponsor asks. The Sponsorship Lead does not wait for a sponsor to say "we didn't see our logo at last night's event." The checklist is completed before every event. The content is briefed to Marketing before the monthly deadline. The quarterly review is booked before the quarter ends. Sponsors who are surprised by things not happening become ex-sponsors. Sponsors who are surprised by things happening before they asked become long-term partners.

Section 05

Renewal Process

Renewal should never be a surprise conversation. The Sponsorship Lead manages each relationship so that renewal is the obvious and expected next step — not a negotiation that starts from scratch at the end of every quarter.

01
Flag renewal 45 days before quarter end
Notion reminder set at the start of every sponsor's agreement: 45 days before expiry, Sponsorship Lead reviews the relationship. Is the sponsor happy? Are all benefits being delivered? Is there any open issue? If yes — it must be resolved before the renewal conversation. A renewal conversation that starts with an unresolved complaint is a difficult renewal conversation.
Trigger: Notion auto-reminder at 45 days
Sponsorship LeadNotion reminder
02
Renewal conversation — 30 days before quarter end
First right of renewal is offered 30 days before the quarter ends — as defined in every agreement. Sponsorship Lead initiates the conversation: "We'd love to continue the partnership into next quarter — are you in?" For Founding Sponsors, CEO is on this call. The conversation starts from the value delivered this quarter, not from the price. If the sponsor wants to adjust their tier up or down, that is discussed here — not left to the last minute.
Trigger: 30 days before quarter end
Sponsorship Lead (+ CEO for Founding)Call or in-person
03
Renewal agreed → new agreement drafted and signed before current one expires
If the sponsor renews, a new agreement is prepared from the template. Even if terms are identical, a new agreement is signed. The old agreement's end date is the new agreement's start date — no gap, no ambiguity. COO reviews and approves. Payment confirmed before new quarter begins. Compliance updated.
Trigger: Renewal agreed
Sponsorship Lead → COOGoogle Drive — new signed agreement
04
Sponsor does not renew — graceful close and pipeline activation
If a sponsor does not renew, Sponsorship Lead thanks them sincerely — by phone or in person, not by WhatsApp message. Asks one question: "Is there anything we could have done differently?" Logs the answer in Notion. Removes them from active sponsor systems. Notifies Marketing and Logistics that this sponsor's benefits end at the agreement date. Sponsor pipeline is immediately reviewed — Sponsorship Lead must identify a replacement candidate within 14 days of a non-renewal.
Trigger: Sponsor confirms non-renewal
Sponsorship LeadNotion — sponsor record closed
Section 06

Sponsorship Boundaries

These are the lines Terra does not cross for any sponsor at any tier. They protect the community's trust in Terra. Once that trust is broken — even once — it is very difficult to rebuild. The Sponsorship Lead knows these boundaries and can articulate them clearly and confidently to any sponsor who pushes.

Hard Boundary 01
No attendee data. Ever.
No sponsor receives any attendee's contact information — not as part of a tier, not as a custom arrangement, not as a "one-time exception." If a sponsor requests this, Sponsorship Lead declines immediately and logs the request as a red flag about their intentions.
How to say it: "Our community members trust Terra with their data. We don't share it — with anyone. That's part of why they keep coming back, which is why you're here."
Hard Boundary 02
No editorial control.
Sponsors do not influence who speaks at Terra events, what topics are discussed, what Terra's programs teach, or what Terra publishes. If a sponsor says "we'd prefer you didn't invite [speaker]" or "we'd like the workshop to cover [topic]," Sponsorship Lead declines and explains why.
How to say it: "Our content is chosen by our community's needs, not by our sponsors. That independence is what makes our events worth sponsoring."
Hard Boundary 03
No speaking slot in exchange for sponsorship.
Speaker selection and sponsorship are permanently separate tracks. A sponsor can apply to speak through the normal speaker pipeline — their application is assessed on merit, not on their financial contribution to Terra. This is non-negotiable.
Hard Boundary 04
No exclusivity that restricts Terra's programs.
A sponsor cannot prevent Terra from inviting speakers, running programs, or forming partnerships — even if those entities compete with the sponsor. Exclusivity in branding is acceptable. Exclusivity in operations is not.

Section 09

Operating Rules

Non-Negotiable Operating Rules — Sponsorship Department
01No sponsor receives attendee contact data. Not as a tier benefit, not as a custom arrangement, not as a one-time exception. Any request for attendee data is a red flag that is logged and reported to COO. This boundary is the foundation of community trust — it is not negotiable for any amount of money.
02No agreement is signed without COO written approval. No agreement for a Founding Sponsor is signed without CEO approval. The Sponsorship Lead presents, COO and CEO decide. This is not bureaucracy — it is accountability for the commitments Terra makes.
03A sponsor is not considered active until payment is confirmed by COO. Benefits do not begin until payment clears. No exceptions. The agreement defines this — and Sponsorship Lead reminds every sponsor of it before the quarter begins.
04Fulfilment is tracked proactively. Sponsorship Lead reviews the fulfilment tracker every week. Any benefit that is late is addressed immediately — not at the monthly check-in. Sponsors who receive their benefits early or on time renew. Sponsors who have to chase do not.
05The pipeline maintains a minimum of 3 qualified prospects at all times. When a sponsor does not renew, the pipeline must produce a replacement within 14 days. A sponsorship department with no pipeline is one non-renewal away from a revenue gap.
06Terra does not accept sponsorship from businesses whose products or services Terra would not recommend to its founders independently. The Right Sponsor test is applied before any prospect enters the pipeline. If a sponsor is only valuable to Terra financially and not valuable to Terra's founders, they are not the right sponsor.
07Sponsorship and speaking are permanently separate tracks. A sponsor who also wants to speak applies through the standard speaker pipeline. Their application is assessed on merit only. Sponsorship Lead communicates this clearly to every sponsor who raises it — before it becomes an expectation.
08Co-branded content is approved by Marketing Lead before the sponsor sees it, and approved by the sponsor before it is published. Both approvals are required. The Marketing Lead's approval protects Terra's voice. The sponsor's approval protects the relationship.
09The quarterly sponsor report is sent within 7 days of quarter end — whether the quarter went well or not. If a benefit was missed, it is acknowledged in the report with an explanation and a remediation offer. Transparency in delivery builds more renewal trust than a perfect quarter that goes unreported.
10Terra is never dependent on a single sponsor for more than 40% of sponsorship revenue. This concentration limit protects Terra's operational independence. A sponsor who represents 60% of sponsorship revenue has disproportionate leverage over the organisation — even if they never exercise it explicitly. Diversification is protection.
11When a sponsor does not renew, the Sponsorship Lead closes the relationship by phone or in person — not by message. One question is asked: "Is there anything we could have done differently?" The answer is logged. Patterns in non-renewal reasons drive improvements to the offering.
12The Sponsorship Lead trains a deputy who can manage the full pipeline cycle — identification to renewal — independently. Sponsor relationships are relationship assets, not personal property. They survive leadership transitions if the systems and records are maintained well.
"The right sponsor is one our founders would thank us for introducing."
