Logistics &
Events Operations

Logistics is the invisible architecture of every Terra experience. When it works, nobody notices — they just feel the quality of the event. When it fails, it is the only thing anyone remembers. This department owns the physical reality of every Terra event: the venue, the crew, the setup, the run of show, and the reset. Nothing left to chance. Nothing left to the day.

The Logistics Mandate

What Logistics Owns

Logistics does not run the event — the department lead runs the event. Logistics makes it possible for the department lead to focus entirely on the human experience by guaranteeing that every physical and operational element is handled. The department lead should never be thinking about the microphone or the registration desk on event day.

The Standard
Every Terra event — a workshop in Canggu, a School session in Ubud, a quarterly kick-off — should feel effortless to the people in the room. Effortless is not accidental. It is the result of every logistical detail being resolved before the room fills. The Logistics Lead's success is measured by how invisible their work is on the day.
Logistics Owns
  • Venue
    Sourcing, booking, confirming, and managing the relationship with every venue Terra uses
  • Physical setup
    Seating, AV, registration desk, signage, sponsor visibility
  • Crew coordination
    Briefing, assigning, and supporting Events Crew volunteers on the day
  • Venue reset
    Leaving every venue in better condition than it was found
Logistics Does Not Own
  • Event content
    The speaker, the agenda, the Q&A — that is the department lead's domain
  • Attendee registration data
    Compliance Crew collects and owns the data. Logistics sets up the desk.
  • Marketing assets
    The banner design, the event graphic — that is Marketing's output
  • Speaker relationship
    The department lead manages the speaker. Logistics manages the speaker's arrival logistics.
The Handoffs
  • From Workshops / School / Mentorship
    Brief received by T-20. Logistics acknowledges within 24h.
  • To Marketing
    Venue confirmed → Logistics notifies Marketing so event posts can go out
  • To Compliance
    Attendance sheet and registration desk handed to Compliance Crew on event day
  • To COO
    Any venue, crew, or day-of issue that cannot be resolved within 30 minutes
Section 01

Brief Intake Process

Every event Logistics manages begins with a written brief from the requesting department. No brief, no guarantee of logistics support. The brief is the Logistics Lead's contract for delivery.

01
Brief received from department lead by T-20
Workshops, School, and Mentorship send their logistics briefs by the 20th of the preceding month for all events in the following month. The brief must be complete: event date, time, expected attendance, venue preference (or "Logistics to source"), AV requirements, crew needed, special setup instructions, speaker arrival requirements, and any sponsor visibility requirements.
Trigger: 20th of each month (standard) or minimum 14 days before event (urgent)
Department Lead sendsNotion brief template
02
Logistics Lead acknowledges and checks brief completeness within 24h
Within 24 hours of receiving a brief, Logistics Lead confirms receipt and either accepts it as complete or returns it with a clear list of what's missing. Incomplete briefs are not started. The Logistics Lead does not guess at missing information — they request it. One follow-up. If no response in 48h, escalated to COO.
Trigger: Brief received
Logistics LeadNotion — brief status updated
03
Venue confirmation initiated immediately
As soon as a brief is accepted, Logistics Lead contacts the venue. For established Terra venues (Seminyak, Canggu, Ubud regulars), this is a WhatsApp confirmation to the venue contact. For new venues, a site visit or call is required before confirming. Venue is confirmed in writing — WhatsApp message counts if screenshot is saved in Notion. Confirmation must be obtained by T-7.
Trigger: Brief accepted
Logistics LeadVenue contact
04
Venue confirmed → notify Marketing and department lead
Once venue is locked, Logistics Lead sends one message to both Marketing Lead and the department lead: venue name, address, confirmed capacity, parking notes. Marketing needs this to finalise promotional posts (venue address in bio/stories). Department lead needs this for speaker communication. One message, two recipients, same information.
Trigger: Venue confirmed (must be by T-7)
Logistics Lead → Marketing + Dept LeadWhatsApp
05
Crew assigned and briefed by T-24
Logistics Lead assigns Events Crew volunteers from the available pool, confirms their attendance, and sends the crew brief by T-24. Every crew member receives their brief via WhatsApp in the crew group: arrival time, role on the day, specific tasks, what to wear, who to report to, and what to do if they have a problem. No crew member arrives on the day without knowing their role.
Trigger: By T-24 for all events
Logistics LeadWhatsApp crew group
Section 02

Venue Management

Terra operates across three areas of Bali. Each has a preferred venue profile. Logistics maintains a live venue database with contacts, capacity, AV capability, and cost. No event is confirmed at a venue that hasn't been physically visited by the Logistics Lead or a deputy.

Seminyak
High-footfall · Accessible · First-time attendees
  • ProfileRestaurant private rooms, hotel event spaces, co-working venues with evening availability
  • Capacity30–60 standing, 20–40 seated. Minimum 40 standing for workshops.
  • AV needsMicrophone (wired or wireless), projector or large screen, reliable WiFi
  • ParkingVenue or adjacent street. Must be noted in event communications.
  • PreferenceVenues with existing relationship with Terra or familiar with event format
Canggu
Community core · Repeat attendees · Deeper topics
  • ProfileCo-working spaces, beach clubs with function rooms, villa event spaces
  • Capacity30–50 for workshops. Terra's strongest demographic density in this area.
  • AV needsSame as Seminyak. Audio quality is higher priority in Canggu — beach noise can be a factor.
  • ParkingMore challenging — must confirm parking situation explicitly in event communications.
  • PreferenceCo-working partners. More community-embedded, less commercial.
Ubud
Purpose-driven · Discovery-rich · Different ICP
  • ProfileBoutique venues, wellness-adjacent spaces, small hotel event rooms
  • Capacity20–35. Smaller, more intimate — appropriate for the Ubud founder profile.
  • AV needsSmaller room — microphone sometimes optional depending on acoustics. Always test.
  • ParkingEasier than Canggu. Still noted in communications.
  • PreferenceVenues with aesthetic alignment to Terra's brand. Ubud attendees are visually discerning.
Venue Sourcing Process — For New Venues
01
Identify candidate venue from network or Terra sponsor pipeline
New venues come from: the Terra community (founder owns or manages a space), the Sponsorship department's venue partners, or the Logistics Lead's direct prospecting. Every candidate is logged in the Venue Database in Notion before contact is made.
Logistics LeadNotion venue database
02
Site visit and technical check
Logistics Lead or deputy physically visits every new venue before it is used. Checks: capacity, AV equipment and quality, power socket availability, WiFi reliability, bathroom facilities, registration desk space, parking situation, loading/access for setup, and aesthetic alignment with Terra's brand. If any of these fail a minimum standard, the venue is not used.
Logistics LeadSite visit notes in Notion
03
Negotiate terms and add to venue database
Terms agreed: cost per event (or partnership arrangement via Sponsorship), booking notice required, what is included (AV, tables, chairs, staff), cancellation policy, and the venue contact for all future communication. All terms documented in Notion. Venue added with status "Active" and all fields complete.
Logistics Lead (+ Sponsorship if partnership)Notion venue database

Section 04

Crew Coordination

Events Crew volunteers are the hands of Logistics. Their success is determined by how well they are briefed, not by how capable they are. The Logistics Lead's job before the event is to remove every possible reason for a crew member to be confused on the day.

Crew Briefing — T-24 Process
What Every Crew Brief Contains
  • Event name and date
    Specific enough that there is no ambiguity about which event this is
  • Venue address and parking
    Full address. Nearest landmark. Parking instructions. Google Maps link.
  • Arrival time
    T-60min from event start. Never "around 5" — always "5:00pm sharp."
  • Specific role
    Registration desk / AV / setup / networking support / breakdown. One role, clearly stated.
  • What to wear
    Terra standard (if applicable) or smart casual. Never needs to be guessed.
  • Who to report to on arrival
    Logistics Lead name and WhatsApp number.
  • What to do if there's a problem
    Contact Logistics Lead immediately. Do not improvise.
Crew Pool Management
  • Minimum pool size
    8 active Events Crew volunteers at all times. Allows for 3 per event with backup capacity.
  • Availability tracking
    Logistics Lead polls crew for monthly availability on the 1st of each month. Notion tracker updated. No last-minute scrambles.
  • Backup protocol
    Every event has 1 identified backup crew member who has confirmed availability and will be called if a primary crew member cancels.
  • Crew welfare
    Crew members get food and drinks at every event they work. Non-negotiable. They are giving their time. They eat.
  • Crew feedback
    Logistics Lead checks in with crew after each event — one question: "What would have made tonight easier?" Answers logged in Notion. Patterns drive process improvements.
Section 05

Escalation Matrix

Every scenario that can go wrong on event day has a protocol. The Logistics Lead does not improvise — they execute the protocol. If the protocol does not exist, they escalate to COO. Unknown problems become known protocols within 24 hours.

Critical
Speaker cancels inside 24 hours
Logistics Lead immediately notifies department lead. Department lead decides: find a replacement speaker (Workshop Director's contact list), convert to a discussion format with the audience, or postpone. Logistics Lead supports whichever decision is made — does not make the call. Marketing is notified for any communications change.
→ Decision: Department Lead. Support: Logistics Lead. Comms: Marketing.
Critical
Venue cancels or is unavailable on the day
Logistics Lead immediately activates backup venue protocol. Every event has a pre-identified backup venue (logged in Notion) in the same area. Logistics Lead contacts backup venue, confirms availability, and notifies department lead within 15 minutes. If no backup venue can be secured within 30 minutes, event is postponed. Marketing updates all communications. COO informed.
→ Logistics Lead owns resolution. COO notified within 30 min.
Critical
AV failure at event start or during event
For microphone failure: Logistics Lead has a backup handheld mic or portable speaker in the events kit. Speaker delivers without mic if room is small enough (under 25 people). For projector failure: speaker presents without slides — all speakers are briefed to be able to deliver their talk without visual aids. Logistics Lead manages the transition calmly and does not draw attention to it. Department lead keeps energy in the room.
→ Logistics Lead resolves. Department Lead maintains energy. Audience should not feel it as a problem.
Amber
Crew member does not arrive
Logistics Lead calls backup crew member immediately. If backup is unavailable, Logistics Lead absorbs the role personally. Department lead is not told unless it affects the event experience — it is Logistics' problem to solve invisibly. If the same crew member misses two events without communication, Volunteer Lead is informed.
→ Logistics Lead resolves. Volunteer Lead informed if pattern.
Amber
Attendance significantly exceeds room capacity
Registration desk captures all attendees. Logistics Lead assesses whether additional seating is possible within the venue safely. If not: standing capacity is managed, comfort items adjusted (open windows/doors for air, water available). Department lead is informed at T-5min before event starts. Excess attendees are not turned away — they are accommodated gracefully. Waitlist note logged for next event.
→ Logistics Lead manages physically. Department Lead informed.
Amber
Venue issue during event (power cut, noise, other)
Logistics Lead handles the venue side directly — talks to venue management, does not pull department lead out of the event unless absolutely necessary. Power: use portable lighting if available. Noise: close doors, adjust seating, move room if feasible. If the issue cannot be resolved within 10 minutes and it materially affects the event, department lead is informed and makes the call on continuing vs. adapting.
→ Logistics Lead manages venue. Only escalates to department lead if unresolvable.
Amber
Sponsor materials or visibility not set up correctly
During setup, Logistics Lead checks sponsor requirements from the brief. If materials were not delivered, Sponsorship Lead is contacted immediately. If materials are present but setup is unclear, Logistics Lead contacts Sponsorship Lead for guidance. Sponsor visibility is never improvised — it is either done correctly or the sponsor is informed before the event that it could not be executed as planned.
→ Logistics Lead contacts Sponsorship Lead. Never improvises sponsor placement.
Amber
Registration data not being captured properly
Logistics Lead notices Compliance Crew not capturing all attendees. Logistics Lead privately reinforces the standard with the crew member — not in front of attendees. If the issue persists, Logistics Lead positions a crew member at the exit to capture anyone who was missed. After the event: issue logged in Notion and shared with Volunteer Lead for training follow-up.
→ Logistics Lead corrects in real time. Volunteer Lead informed post-event.
The Escalation Principle
Logistics Lead resolves everything that can be resolved without breaking the event's energy for the department lead and attendees. The department lead is told about a problem only when they need to make a decision or when the problem has already affected the event. This is not about hiding things — it is about protecting the person who is responsible for the room's energy from being pulled into a logistics problem that Logistics should own.
Section 06

Post-Event Close

The event is not over when the last attendee leaves. It is over when the venue is reset, the data is handed to Compliance, and the Logistics Lead has logged the event in Notion. Every time.

01
Full crew venue reset
All crew stay for reset — no crew member leaves before reset is complete without explicit permission from Logistics Lead. Furniture returned to original position. All Terra materials (signage, registration desk items, sponsor materials) packed and taken. Rubbish cleared. Floor cleared. Venue left visibly cleaner than arrival. This is the rule that protects Terra's venue relationships.
Trigger: Last attendee leaves
All Events CrewIn-person
02
Venue close walkthrough with venue contact
Before leaving, Logistics Lead does a walkthrough with the venue contact (manager, owner, or designated staff). Confirm the venue is in the agreed condition. If there is any damage or issue, it is acknowledged immediately — not hidden. Terra's credibility with venues is built on honest communication, not avoidance. Venue contact signs off or provides a message confirming close.
Trigger: Reset complete
Logistics Lead + venue contactIn-person walkthrough
03
Confirm data handoff to Compliance — same night
Compliance Crew confirms to Logistics Lead that attendance data has been uploaded to the master Google Sheet. Logistics Lead does not close the event in Notion until this confirmation is received. If data upload is not confirmed by midnight on event night, Logistics Lead follows up with Compliance Crew directly — this is the one non-negotiable handoff in the system.
Trigger: Venue close complete
Compliance confirms → Logistics LeadWhatsApp confirmation
04
Send event close message to department lead
Same night. One message: "Venue reset complete, data confirmed with Compliance, [any notable issues logged]." Department lead needs to know their event is fully closed before they sleep. If there were any issues on the night, they are named here — not discovered later.
Trigger: All above complete
Logistics Lead → Department LeadWhatsApp
05
Log event in Notion — within 24h
Event record updated: attendance (confirmed with Compliance), venue performance (reuse / adjust / retire), AV status, crew performance notes, any issues and how they were resolved, and one recommendation for next time at this venue. This record is institutional memory. The Logistics Lead who runs the next event at this venue reads this note before arriving.
Trigger: Within 24h of event
Logistics LeadNotion event record

Section 09

Operating Rules

Non-Negotiable Operating Rules — Logistics & Events Department
01No event logistics are managed without a complete written brief. Verbal or WhatsApp requests do not trigger logistics support. The brief is submitted, acknowledged, and accepted — in that order.
02Every venue is confirmed in writing by T-7. If not confirmed by T-7, Logistics Lead escalates to COO immediately — not at T-3. Late confirmation compounds risk exponentially.
03Every crew member is briefed by T-24 and must confirm receipt. A crew member who has not confirmed their brief by T-12 is called directly. Confirmation "confirmed" in WhatsApp is the minimum acceptable acknowledgment.
04Crew arrives T-60min. Setup is complete and tested by T-30min. The department lead receives a "setup complete" message before doors open. No exceptions, no "almost ready" at T-5min.
05Every new venue is physically visited by Logistics Lead or a designated deputy before it is used. Site visit checklist completed and logged. Venue not used without this step — regardless of how well it looks on Instagram or how enthusiastically it was recommended.
06Every event has a pre-identified backup venue confirmed in advance. Venue cancellations happen in Bali. The backup is never improvised on the day.
07Events Crew are fed at every event they work. This is not optional and is not budget-dependent. Crew welfare is a logistics responsibility.
08Logistics Lead does not pull the department lead into a logistics problem unless the department lead needs to make a decision. The department lead's energy is the event's energy. Protect it.
09The venue is left cleaner than arrival. Every time. This single rule is what maintains Terra's access to good venues at favourable terms. Venue relationships are multi-year assets.
10Compliance data upload is confirmed same night before Logistics Lead closes the event. This is the last step of every event. Without it, the event is not complete.
11Every escalation scenario has a protocol. If a problem arises that has no protocol, Logistics Lead resolves it and then writes the protocol within 24 hours. Unknown scenarios become known processes — never repeated unknowns.
12The Logistics Lead trains a deputy who can run any event independently — brief intake to venue reset — without the Logistics Lead present. The run-of-show template and the venue database are the primary handover tools.
