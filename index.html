Department 01 of 09
Mentorship Program
Lead: Program Director
"The heart of Terra. Everything else exists to support this."
Why This Department Exists
The 8-week program is why Terra exists.

The Mentorship Program connects founders who are building with founders who have built. It creates the structure for honest conversations, real accountability, and practical growth. Without this running well, nothing else in Terra matters. The Program Director owns the quality, consistency, and impact of every cohort — from the first application to the final session.

Core Responsibilities
  • Run mentee application and selection process each cycle
  • Match mentees to mentors based on stage, industry, and need
  • Deliver all 8 weekly group mastermind sessions
  • Onboard new mentors — brief, align, and support them
  • Track mentee progress and flag disengagement early
  • Collect feedback at week 4 and week 8
  • Manage daily accountability task cadence
  • Issue program completion recognition
  • Document cohort learnings after each cycle
  • Identify and invite successful mentees to become mentors
What Success Looks Like
  • Completion rate above 75%
    Mentees who start the program finish it. Drop-off is tracked and addressed mid-cycle, not after.
  • Every mentor feels supported
    Mentors know what's expected. They have what they need. They stay.
  • Measurable founder progress
    At week 8, founders can name one specific thing that changed in their business because of Terra.
  • Program runs without COO in the room
    The Program Director owns the full cycle. No escalation needed for standard operations.

Department 02 of 09
Events
Lead: Events Director
"The front door. Where founders first experience what Terra feels like."
Why This Department Exists
Events are how people discover Terra and decide to trust it.

A Terra event is not a networking night. It is a curated experience that shows founders — before they've committed to anything — what it feels like to be in a room full of people who actually want to help each other. The Events Director creates that feeling consistently, every time. From the speaker to the follow-up message, every touchpoint reflects Terra's promise.

Core Responsibilities
  • Plan and execute monthly Terra events end-to-end
  • Source, brief, and confirm speakers minimum 2 weeks ahead
  • Build and manage speaker waitlist pipeline
  • Create event promotional assets with Marketing
  • Manage registration and attendee communication
  • Brief and assign event crew with Logistics
  • Host and MC every event
  • Follow up with new attendees within 48 hours
  • Capture and share post-event content
  • Send thank you to speakers and venue partners
  • Document event learnings for next cycle
What Success Looks Like
  • Every attendee knows what Terra is before they leave
    Not just that it exists — what the program is, how to apply, and why it's free.
  • 30%+ of event attendees apply to the mentorship program
    Events are the top of the funnel. They convert when the experience is right.
  • Events run on time, on brief, without crisis
    Logistics handled. Speaker confirmed. Crew briefed. No last-minute scramble.
  • Speaker pipeline always 3 events ahead
    Never scrambling for next month's speaker. Always building the relationship before you need it.

Department 03 of 09
School
Lead: School Director
"Structured learning for founders who want to go deeper."
Why This Department Exists
Some founders need more than mentorship. They need a curriculum.

Terra School provides structured, modular learning for founders who want to build specific skills — finance, operations, leadership, marketing. It's practical, taught by practitioners, and designed to be completed alongside running a business. The School Director owns the curriculum quality, teacher relationships, and student experience from enrollment to completion.

Core Responsibilities
  • Design and maintain curriculum structure each cycle
  • Recruit, vet, and brief session teachers
  • Run weekly school sessions on schedule
  • Launch enrollment campaigns each cycle
  • Run student orientation at cycle start
  • Track attendance and flag disengagement
  • Assign and review module tasks
  • Conduct mid-cycle review and adjust
  • Approve completions and issue certificates
  • Collect student feedback per module
  • Build teacher pipeline 2 cycles ahead
What Success Looks Like
  • Students finish what they start
    Completion rate above 70%. Mid-cycle check-in catches disengagement before it becomes dropout.
  • Teachers come back
    A teacher who has a good experience refers the next teacher. The pipeline self-fills.
  • Content is actionable, not theoretical
    Every session ends with something a founder can do this week. Not just something they know.
  • School and Mentorship cross-refer
    Mentors recommend School to mentees with specific skill gaps. School graduates become mentorship candidates.

Department 04 of 09
Volunteers
Lead: Community Lead
"The people who make Terra run. Treated like the leaders they are."
Why This Department Exists
Terra runs on human energy. The Volunteer team protects and grows it.

Volunteers are not free labor. They are people who choose to give their time because Terra means something to them. The Community Lead's job is to make sure that choice stays meaningful — by matching people to roles that fit them, recognizing their contribution, watching for burnout, and creating a clear pathway from volunteer to leader. When volunteers feel like they're growing, they stay. When they feel like they're just doing tasks, they disappear.

Core Responsibilities
  • Run ongoing volunteer recruitment campaigns
  • Screen applicants and match to right department
  • Run volunteer orientation every new intake
  • Maintain live volunteer roster with roles and availability
  • Monthly 1:1 check-in with every active volunteer
  • Proactively flag burnout risk before it becomes departure
  • Identify volunteers ready for leadership roles
  • Maintain succession pipeline across all departments
  • Buddy system for all new volunteers in first 30 days
  • Manage respectful volunteer offboarding
What Success Looks Like
  • Volunteers stay for more than one cycle
    Retention above 60% cycle-on-cycle. High turnover means the experience isn't right — not that volunteers aren't committed enough.
  • Every department has its succession filled
    No department is one departure away from a gap. The roster shows who's being trained for what.
  • Volunteers refer other volunteers
    The best sign the experience is good: people bring their friends.
  • Zero surprise departures
    Monthly check-ins mean burnout and disengagement are caught early. No one disappears without a conversation first.

Department 05 of 09
Marketing
Lead: Marketing Lead
"Tell the Terra story. Bring the right people in."
Why This Department Exists
Marketing's job is to make sure the right founders find Terra before they need it.

Terra's marketing is not about followers or impressions. It's about reaching founders in Bali who are building something, feeling isolated, and don't yet know that Terra exists. Everything the Marketing Lead creates — every post, every reel, every message — should make that founder feel like Terra was made for them. Quality over volume. Story over promotion. Community over broadcasting.

Core Responsibilities
  • Build and execute monthly social content calendar
  • Create promotional assets for every event
  • Manage Terra Telegram channel and WhatsApp community
  • Run mentorship application campaigns each cycle
  • Track and report marketing funnel metrics monthly
  • Build and maintain speaker waitlist with Events
  • Design program and event graphics
  • Respond to DMs and community inquiries within 24 hours
  • Capture and publish post-event content
What Success Looks Like
  • Event attendance grows cycle on cycle
    Not chasing viral moments. Steady, compounding reach to the right people in Bali.
  • Mentorship applications fill every cohort
    The application pipeline never runs dry. Marketing knows when enrollment opens and starts campaigns 3 weeks before.
  • Community channels feel alive, not broadcast
    People reply, share, engage. Not just consume. The channel is a community, not a newsletter.
  • Every piece of content passes the Terra test
    Would a founder in Bali feel like this was made for them? If not, it doesn't go out.

Department 06 of 09
Logistics
Lead: Operations Lead
"When Logistics is working, nobody notices. That's the point."
Why This Department Exists
Great events don't happen by accident. Logistics makes them inevitable.

The Operations Lead ensures that every Terra event runs smoothly — venue confirmed, equipment ready, crew briefed, timeline tight. When Logistics does its job, the Events Director can focus on the experience instead of the chaos. The best compliment for this role is that nobody noticed anything went wrong — because nothing did.

Core Responsibilities
  • Confirm venue minimum 72 hours before every event
  • Build and maintain venue relationship pipeline
  • Manage equipment checklist and ensure availability
  • Build crew rotation schedule across events
  • Assign specific crew roles per event and brief them
  • On-site event management from setup to breakdown
  • Manage printing and physical materials per event
  • Track and maintain supply inventory
What Success Looks Like
  • Zero last-minute venue problems
    72-hour confirmation is the non-negotiable. If it hasn't happened by then, escalate immediately.
  • Crew knows their role before they arrive
    No one is figuring out what to do on the day. Every crew member has been briefed with a specific role and timeline.
  • Equipment checklist completed before every event
    Never scrambling for a cable or a screen on the day. The checklist is the system.
  • Venue partners want to work with Terra again
    We treat every venue like a long-term relationship. We leave it better than we found it. We say thank you.

Department 07 of 09
Compliance
Lead: Systems & Data Lead
"The system that keeps Terra honest with itself."
Why This Department Exists
What gets measured gets maintained. Compliance keeps Terra from drifting.

Terra runs on trust — and trust requires consistency. The Systems & Data Lead tracks attendance, flags policy breaches, monitors volunteer wellbeing signals, and submits weekly reports that give leadership a clear view of how the community is actually functioning. This is not about enforcement. It's about making sure small problems are caught before they become big ones.

Core Responsibilities
  • Maintain master attendance dashboard — updated within 24h of each session
  • Send absence alerts to relevant program leads
  • Maintain policy breach log with dates and context
  • Escalate repeated breaches to COO with full record
  • Submit weekly compliance report every Monday
  • Monitor volunteer policy adherence across departments
  • Flag burnout risk signals to Community Lead immediately
What Success Looks Like
  • Weekly report lands every Monday without chasing
    Consistency is the job. One missed report breaks the system's reliability for everyone who depends on it.
  • No problem is a surprise to leadership
    Attendance drops, policy breaches, burnout signals — leadership hears about them from Compliance before they become visible problems.
  • Data is clean, current, and accessible
    Any department lead can check the dashboard and trust what they see. No outdated records. No missing entries.
  • The role feels like support, not surveillance
    The Systems Lead's tone matters. This role serves the community by keeping it healthy — it's not policing people.

Department 08 of 09
Sponsorship
Lead: Sponsorship Lead
"The fuel that keeps everything free. Earned through alignment, not sales."
Why This Department Exists
Terra is free because sponsors believe in what it does. That relationship must be earned and protected.

Sponsorship is not sales. It's partnership. The Sponsorship Lead finds businesses and organizations whose values align with Terra — who want to support a founder community not because it's a marketing channel, but because they genuinely believe in what Terra is building. The relationship is maintained with transparency, delivered value, and honest reporting. Sponsors who feel like partners renew. Sponsors who feel like customers leave.

Core Responsibilities
  • Define and maintain sponsorship packages and tiers
  • Secure monthly sponsor commitments to cover operational costs
  • Allocate sponsor funds to events and marketing with COO
  • Build and maintain active sponsor pipeline
  • Run outreach to prospective sponsors consistently
  • Deliver sponsor visibility commitments at every event
  • Send monthly sponsor impact report
  • Renew sponsor relationships before they expire
  • Coordinate sponsor requirements with Logistics and Events
  • Maintain signed sponsor agreements log
  • Issue post-event sponsor thank you and recap
What Success Looks Like
  • Operational costs covered every month
    Terra's core operations — venue, materials, marketing — never stop because funding ran out. The pipeline is always 2 months ahead.
  • Sponsors renew without being chased
    The monthly report and consistent delivery mean sponsors already know the value before the renewal conversation.
  • Sponsors refer other sponsors
    The best sign a sponsor relationship is working: they bring a colleague who wants in too.
  • Terra's integrity is never compromised for a sponsor
    No sponsor gets influence over Terra's content, community, or values. Sponsorship buys visibility and association — nothing else.

Department 09 of 09
COO
Chief Operating Officer
"Builds the system. Protects the purpose. Gets out of the way."
Why This Role Exists
The COO's job is to make themselves unnecessary — one department at a time.

The COO holds the architecture of Terra — the systems, the standards, the north star. But the measure of a good COO is not how much they do. It's how well Terra runs when they step back. Every decision the COO makes should be building toward a Terra that operates independently, keeps its values intact, and grows without depending on any single person — including the COO.

Core Responsibilities
  • Define and protect Terra's purpose, values, and non-negotiables
  • Ensure all 9 departments have clear leads and working systems
  • Hold final accountability for all critical decisions
  • Weekly review of compliance report and department health
  • Approve sponsorship packages and fund allocation
  • Support department leads — remove blockers, not do their work
  • Oversee succession planning across all departments
  • Represent Terra externally — partnerships, collaborations
  • Quarterly review of Terra's overall direction and health
  • Document decisions and rationale for institutional memory
What Success Looks Like
  • Terra runs for 30 days without the COO and nothing breaks
    If this isn't possible yet, it's the most important thing to work toward. Every other metric is secondary.
  • Every department lead makes decisions independently
    Leads come to the COO for support, not permission. The difference matters enormously.
  • Terra's values are visible in every department's behavior
    Not because the COO is watching. Because the system rewards it.
  • The community is growing and the trust is intact
    More founders. More mentors. More impact. Zero compromises on the things that make Terra worth joining.

A Note on COO Succession
The COO role is the last to be succeeded — but it must be planned. As Terra grows, the COO trains one person who understands the full architecture: the purpose document, the department playbooks, the values, and the non-negotiables. The goal is a Terra where the COO can step into a strategic role and Terra's operations continue without interruption.
