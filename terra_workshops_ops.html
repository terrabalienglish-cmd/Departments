Workshops
Operations

Workshops are Terra's weekly presence in Bali. Three locations. Free every time. The front door for every founder who will one day join the mentorship program. This manual defines every process, system, and document that makes workshops run consistently — from monthly calendar to post-event follow-up. Nothing left to memory.

Context

Three Locations, One Standard

Every workshop at every location must feel like a Terra workshop. Same quality, same energy, same CTA. The location changes. The standard doesn't.

Seminyak
Location 01
High-footfall area. Most accessible to international founders and digital nomads. Best for broad reach and first-time Terra attendees. Venue partner should have strong F&B or co-working identity. Parking and transport access matters here.
Canggu
Location 02
Terra's strongest existing community density. Most repeat attendees. Best for community deepening and word-of-mouth growth. Speaker topics can go deeper here — audience is familiar with the format and trusts the room.
Ubud
Location 03
Different founder profile — more purpose-driven, wellness-adjacent, creative entrepreneurs. Tone can be slightly more reflective. Audience discovery rate is higher here — many first-time Terra attendees. Venue should reflect the environment.
The Journey Principle
Workshops are not isolated events. They are chapters in an ongoing story. The Workshop Director designs the monthly and quarterly arc — themes build on each other, speakers are chosen to create a through-line, and attendees who come to multiple events feel like they're on a journey, not just attending events. This is the difference between a community and a mailing list.

Process — Monthly Calendar Planning
01
Set the monthly theme
By the 10th of each month, Workshop Director sets the theme for the following month. Theme informs speaker selection and Marketing content direction. Examples: "Revenue Traction", "Building a Team in Bali", "Founder Mental Health", "Distribution Strategy". Theme should connect to the quarterly arc.
Trigger: 10th of each month, for the following month
Workshop DirectorNotion
02
Confirm speakers for all 3 events from pipeline
Pull from the speaker waitlist in Notion. Match speakers to locations based on their topic, audience fit, and which location suits their profile. Confirm availability. If the pipeline is below 3 events ahead, run speaker outreach immediately — this is a system alert, not a task to do later.
Trigger: Monthly theme set
Workshop DirectorNotion speaker pipeline
03
Send speaker confirmation and brief
Formal confirmation email with: event date, location, format (30-min talk + 15-min Q&A), audience profile, the one thing they should leave the audience with, and what not to do (no pitch, no product promotion). Attach the speaker brief document.
Trigger: Speaker confirmed verbally
Workshop DirectorGmail template
04
Brief Logistics on all 3 events
One brief per event. Sent by the 20th. Covers: date, time, location (venue if pre-confirmed or venue requirements if not yet booked), expected attendance, AV needs, crew needed, registration setup, and any specific requirements. Logistics cannot work with "TBC" — give them everything they need to execute.
Trigger: By 20th of preceding month
Workshop Director → LogisticsNotion brief
05
Brief Marketing on all 3 events
One brief per event. Sent by the 20th. Covers: event date, location, speaker name and topic, the story angle for this event (not just the logistics), the audience it's right for, and the CTA. Marketing needs a story, not just a flyer brief.
Trigger: Same day as Logistics brief
Workshop Director → MarketingNotion brief
06
Review and approve all promotional content before publishing
Workshop Director approves every asset Marketing creates before it goes out. Not for design quality — for story accuracy and message alignment. Review turnaround: within 24 hours of receiving assets. Never let Marketing wait more than 24h for approval.
Trigger: Marketing submits assets for review
Workshop Director approvesNotion or WhatsApp
07
Lock and publish monthly calendar
Once all 3 events are briefed and approved, Workshop Director publishes the monthly schedule to WhatsApp Terra community. No changes after publication unless absolutely unavoidable — and if a change must happen, communicate it immediately with a full explanation.
Trigger: By 20th of preceding month

Process — Speaker Outreach to Confirmation
01
Identify speaker candidate
Sources: Terra community attendees, mentor pool, Workshop Director's network, alumni referrals, LinkedIn, other Bali founder communities. Add to Notion speaker pipeline with: name, contact, topic area, source, and notes. Never approach without knowing what you'd ask them to speak about.
Trigger: Ongoing — not reactive
Workshop DirectorNotion pipeline
02
First approach — warm, specific, no pressure
Not a cold form. A direct message or email that references something specific about them, names the topic you had in mind, describes the Terra audience, and makes it easy to say yes or no. Tone: invitation, not recruitment. Keep it under 100 words.
Trigger: Pipeline below 5 in "Approached" stage
Workshop DirectorGmail / WhatsApp DM
03
Explore topic and fit in a short conversation
20-minute call or voice note exchange. Cover: what they'd speak about, the one thing the audience should leave knowing, whether they've spoken publicly before, location preference. Assess: Is this person right for Terra? Is the topic right for this audience? Trust your instinct — quality over filling a slot.
Trigger: Speaker expresses interest
Workshop DirectorDirect conversation
04
Approve speaker internally
Workshop Director makes the call. Check against: Does this topic serve the monthly theme? Is this person likely to deliver a quality session? Does their profile fit the Terra ICP? No approval by committee — one decision-maker, clear standard. Update Notion stage to "Approved — pending date".
Trigger: Post-conversation assessment
Workshop DirectorNotion
05
Send formal speaker confirmation email with brief
Confirmation email includes: date, time, venue, format (30-min + 15-min Q&A), audience description, the one thing to leave the audience with, what to avoid (no sales pitch, no product promo), what Terra will provide (AV, water, intro, promotion), and what the speaker provides (their talk, 1 headshot, 1-line bio for promo). Attach Speaker Expectations document.
Trigger: Date agreed
Workshop DirectorGmail template
06
Confirm speaker 48h before event
Brief WhatsApp message: confirm time, address, parking/transport info, contact on the day (Logistics Lead's number). Ask if anything has changed. Notify Logistics that speaker is confirmed. This step prevents the worst-case scenario: a no-show speaker.
Trigger: 48 hours before event
Workshop DirectorWhatsApp direct

01
Send thank you within 24h of event
Personal message — not a template. Reference something specific from their talk or the Q&A. Share the content that Marketing captured. Tell them how many attended. Make them feel the impact of what they did.
Trigger: Within 24h post-event
Workshop DirectorGmail
02
Send speaker feedback form
Short Google Form: How was the experience? What could Terra improve for speakers? Would they come back? Would they refer another speaker? Their answer to the last question is your next pipeline entry.

Workshop DirectorGoogle Form
03
Update speaker record in Notion
Log: event date, location, topic, attendance count, quality rating (internal — never shared), feedback summary, whether they'd return. Move to "Alumni" stage. Tag for future cohort invitations if they're a strong mentor candidate.
Trigger: Feedback received or 5 days post-event
Workshop DirectorNotion
Section 03

Workshop Processes

Three processes govern every workshop: pre-event preparation, event-day execution, and post-event follow-up. Each has a clear owner and a clear sequence. Nothing is done by feel on the day.

Process — Pre-Event (T-14 to T-0)
01
T-14: Promotional assets approved and scheduled
Workshop Director has approved all Marketing assets. First announcement post is scheduled. Registration link is live. All information is accurate — date, time, venue, speaker name and topic, and the CTA.
Trigger: 14 days before event
Workshop Director + MarketingNotion approval log
02
T-7: Venue confirmed by Logistics
Workshop Director receives confirmation from Logistics that venue is booked. If not confirmed by T-7, escalate immediately — not T-3. Logistics owns the venue. Workshop Director owns the escalation if it hasn't happened.
Trigger: 7 days before event
Logistics confirms → Workshop DirectorWhatsApp or Notion
03
T-7: Registration reminder goes out
Second announcement post from Marketing. Registration link in the caption. WhatsApp community message. If an event hasn't filled its registration by T-7, Marketing and Workshop Director review the message and adjust — not ignore it.
Trigger: 7 days before event
MarketingWhatsApp + Social
04
T-3: Final reminder post and WhatsApp announcement
Third marketing push. "3 days to go" format. Workshop Director reviews for accuracy before it goes out. Registration link still active. Mention the CTA context — what attendees will walk away with.
Trigger: 3 days before event
Marketing (Workshop Director approves)WhatsApp + Social
05
T-48: Speaker confirmation message sent
Workshop Director sends WhatsApp to speaker: time, venue address, who to contact on the day (Logistics Lead number), parking/transport info, a reminder of format (30 min talk + 15 min Q&A). Brief, friendly, professional.
Trigger: 48 hours before event
Workshop DirectorWhatsApp direct
06
T-24: Crew briefed by Logistics
Logistics briefs every volunteer crew member: their specific role, what time to arrive (always 60 mins before event), the run-of-show, and who to report to. Workshop Director does not manage crew — Logistics does. Workshop Director confirms the brief was sent.
Trigger: 24 hours before event
Logistics (Workshop Director confirms)WhatsApp
07
T-1: Day-of reminder to registered attendees
Marketing sends a "see you tomorrow" message to all registered attendees. Includes: exact venue address, start time, and one-line reminder of what the talk is about. Also goes to WhatsApp community group. Reminder reduces no-shows significantly.
Trigger: Day before event
MarketingEmail + WhatsApp
Process — Event Day
01
T-60min: Crew arrives and sets up
All crew arrive 60 minutes early. Logistics Lead runs setup: AV test, registration desk ready, seating arranged, signage in place, speaker area prepared. Volunteers know their positions before the first attendee walks in.
Trigger: 60 minutes before doors open
Logistics Lead + VolunteersOn-site
02
T-30min: Speaker arrives and is briefed on the day
Workshop Director greets the speaker. Brief walkthrough: stage layout, mic setup, where to stand, how the Q&A will be facilitated, what the MC intro will say. Make the speaker feel prepared and relaxed. Never brief a speaker for the first time on the day — the written brief should have done that work already.
Trigger: 30 minutes before doors open
Workshop DirectorOn-site
03
Doors open: Registration desk captures every attendee
Every attendee checks in at registration. Volunteer collects: name, WhatsApp number (primary), email (optional). QR code option available. Compliance Lead (or assigned volunteer) is responsible for ensuring zero walk-throughs without capture. This data is the funnel — it cannot be missed.
Trigger: Doors open
Volunteer + ComplianceGoogle Sheet (live)
04
Workshop Director opens the event
3–5 minutes maximum. Cover: welcome to Terra, who Terra is for, what tonight is, and why it matters. Not a speech — an invitation. Warm the room before introducing the speaker. Scripted, rehearsed, delivered naturally. Never improvised.
Trigger: Event start time
Workshop DirectorLive
05
Speaker delivers talk (30 minutes)
Workshop Director manages time. Subtle signal at 25 minutes. Hard stop at 30 minutes — protect the Q&A. If the speaker runs over regularly, address it in the post-event debrief, not during the session.
Trigger: After MC intro
Speaker presents · Workshop Director timesLive
06
Workshop Director facilitates Q&A (15 minutes)
Workshop Director holds the mic and calls on attendees. First question is always pre-prepared — never let a Q&A die with silence. If a question goes off-topic or too long, redirect clearly and kindly. Close Q&A at 15 minutes, not when questions run dry.
Trigger: Speaker talk ends
Workshop DirectorLive
07
Deliver Terra CTA — clear, specific, no more than 3 minutes
Workshop Director closes the event with the CTA. One call to action only — do not split attention. This month's CTA: mentorship program application. Show the QR code. Say: "If tonight felt like something you needed, the mentorship program is the next step. Applications are open now." Then stop.
Trigger: Q&A closes
Workshop DirectorLive
08
Networking open — Volunteers circulate and connect
20–30 minutes post-talk networking. Volunteers are not standing at the side — they are in the room, connecting people, having conversations, being Terra. Compliance volunteer captures any additional names missed at registration. Content volunteer captures footage for Marketing.
Trigger: CTA delivered
All volunteers activeOn-site
09
Breakdown and venue reset
Logistics Lead manages full breakdown. Venue left cleaner than it was found. All Terra equipment collected and stored. Venue contact thanked in person. Logistics Lead sends Workshop Director a breakdown confirmation message.
Trigger: Last attendee leaves
Logistics Lead + VolunteersOn-site
Process — Post-Event (Same Night to T+7)
01
Same night: Funnel data uploaded to master sheet
Non-negotiable. Compliance uploads all registration data from the event to the master Google Sheet the same night. Delay = data loss. If Compliance cannot do it that night, Workshop Director does it. The data does not wait.
Trigger: Same night as event
Compliance LeadGoogle Sheet
02
T+24h: Thank you message to speaker
Personal, specific, warm. Reference something from their talk. Share initial attendance count. Tell them their content will be featured. Ask if they have anyone they'd recommend for a future workshop — this is the pipeline conversation, wrapped in gratitude.
Trigger: Within 24 hours
Workshop DirectorGmail
03
T+24h: Event recap content published by Marketing
Reels, photos, and a written recap. Content captures the energy and a key insight from the talk — never a full transcript. Workshop Director reviews before publishing. Content includes the mentorship application link.
Trigger: Within 24 hours
Marketing (Workshop Director approves)Social + WhatsApp
04
T+48h: Follow up with high-interest funnel leads
Workshop Director (or Program Director for mentorship leads) reviews the registration data and identifies anyone who flagged mentorship interest. Personal WhatsApp or email follow-up. Not a mass message — a direct conversation. "I noticed you came last night — would the mentorship program be right for you?"
Trigger: Within 48 hours
Workshop Director + Program DirectorWhatsApp direct
05
T+48h: Speaker feedback form sent
Short Google Form sent to speaker. Covered in the Speaker Pipeline section. Their referral answer feeds the pipeline directly — log it in Notion same day it comes in.
Trigger: 48 hours post-event
Workshop DirectorGoogle Form
06
T+7: Update event record in Notion
Log final attendance count, funnel capture rate, mentorship applications triggered (tracked with Program Director), speaker rating, content performance metrics from Marketing. This data feeds the monthly review. If you're not logging it, you're not learning from it.
Trigger: Within 7 days post-event
Workshop DirectorNotion
07
Monthly review: Did the workshop series hit its metrics?
At the end of each month, Workshop Director reviews all 3 events: attendance trend, funnel conversion rate, speaker quality average, content performance. One-paragraph summary added to Notion monthly log. If any metric is off, the root cause is identified before the next month's calendar is locked.
Trigger: Last day of each month
Workshop DirectorNotion monthly log

Section 06

Operating Rules

These govern how Workshops runs. They exist because every exception creates a precedent. Change them through the monthly review — not by convenience.

Non-Negotiable Operating Rules — Workshops Department
01Three workshops per month minimum. One per location. This is not a target — it is the floor. If a month drops below 3, the root cause is identified and solved before the next month's planning begins.
02The monthly calendar is locked and published by the 20th of the preceding month. No exceptions. Marketing and Logistics cannot function without it.
03The speaker pipeline must always have 3 confirmed events ahead. If it drops below 3, speaker outreach starts that day — not next week.
04No speaker is approved without the Workshop Director's explicit sign-off. Quality over filling a slot. One poor workshop damages the reputation of the next three.
05No promotional content goes out without Workshop Director approval. Marketing executes — Workshop Director approves. Turnaround: within 24 hours. Never hold Marketing up for more than 24h.
06Speaker confirmed 48 hours before every event. No exception. If a speaker cancels inside 48h with no replacement, the workshop is postponed — not run without a speaker.
07Funnel data is uploaded the same night as the event. Not next morning. Not "when I get a chance." The same night. This is a system-critical rule.
08The CTA is delivered at every single event. Every time. Regardless of how "good" the event was. Regardless of who is in the room. The mentorship program is the reason workshops exist.
09Every speaker gets a thank you within 24 hours. Every speaker gets a feedback form. Both are logged in Notion. The speaker relationship is a long-term asset, not a one-night transaction.
10The workshop must feel the same in Ubud as it does in Canggu. Different location. Same quality. Same energy. Same standard. The Workshop Director is responsible for consistency across all 3.
11Nothing important about this department is stored in WhatsApp, personal email, or personal devices. If it's not in Notion or Google Drive, it doesn't exist for Terra.
12The Workshop Director trains 2 successors who can run a full workshop cycle — from monthly planning to post-event logging — independently. The MC script and speaker brief template are the primary handover documents.
