Role 01 of 10
CEO · Founder
Anton — Country Account Lead
"Vision keeper. Final word. The reason Terra exists."
Why this role exists
The CEO holds the vision that everything else serves. Anton founded Terra and defines what it stands for — the non-negotiables, the long-term direction, the culture. The COO builds and runs the system. But the CEO decides what the system is for. This role is also Terra's external face — the person who builds relationships with the broader Terra network across 6 countries, makes strategic decisions about where Terra Bali goes next, and holds final accountability when something important is at stake.
Cross-Department Authority
The CEO has final say on: Terra's purpose and values · structural governance changes · creation or closure of programs · external partnerships · annual vision and capacity · cross-program conflicts that escalate beyond COO level. The CEO is informed on operational matters, not managing them.
Scope of Work
  • Define and hold Terra Bali's annual visionCritical
  • Approve all structural governance changesCritical
  • Approve creation or closure of any programCritical
  • Lead monthly Governance Council meetingHigh
  • Conduct quarterly performance review with COOHigh
  • Annual capacity planning reviewHigh
  • Lead external partnership developmentHigh
  • Resolve cross-program conflicts escalated beyond COOHigh
  • Maintain Terra Bali Operating Manual (with COO)High
  • Bridge relationship with Terra global network (6 countries)High
  • Final approval on all sponsorship packagesHigh
  • Represent Terra at key quarterly kick-off eventsMedium
  • Document strategic decisions for institutional memoryMedium
What Success Looks Like
  • Terra's values are intact, every cycle
    The non-negotiables are non-negotiable. Nothing is for sale. The community trusts the space.
  • The COO and leads operate without needing the CEO daily
    CEO involvement is strategic, not operational. If the CEO is needed daily, the system isn't working.
  • Terra Bali's growth feeds the global network
    Bali becomes the proof of concept that opens conversations with the 8,000-person network across 6 countries.
  • Every major decision is documented
    Terra does not lose its institutional memory when people move on. The operating manual is alive.
  • Annual vision is set, shared, understood
    Every department lead can articulate where Terra is going this year without asking the CEO.

Role 02 of 10
COO
Chief Operating Officer — Parallel Strategic Layer
"Builds the machine. Gets out of its way once it runs."
Why this role exists
The COO translates vision into operating reality. Where the CEO defines what Terra is for, the COO builds the systems, processes, and people architecture that make it function. The COO operates in parallel with the CEO — a strategic partner, not a subordinate — while remaining formally under the CEO's authority on direction-setting decisions. The measure of COO success is not how much they do. It is how well Terra runs when they step back.
Cross-Department Responsibility
The COO holds oversight of all 8 departments. Weekly visibility via compliance report. Direct support to all leads — removing blockers, not doing their work. Final operational decision-maker below CEO level. Owns the department playbook system, succession planning oversight, and fund allocation in partnership with Sponsorship.
Scope of Work
  • Ensure all 8 departments have clear leads and working systemsCritical
  • Hold final operational accountability below CEOCritical
  • Define and protect Terra's operational non-negotiablesCritical
  • Weekly review of compliance report and department healthHigh
  • Approve fund allocation with Sponsorship LeadHigh
  • Oversee succession planning across all departmentsHigh
  • Support department leads — unblock, not replaceHigh
  • Lead quarterly department lead reviewsHigh
  • Represent Terra operationally at external meetingsHigh
  • Build and maintain department playbook systemHigh
  • Cross-department conflict resolution at operational levelHigh
  • Approve department lead promotionsHigh
  • Document all operational decisions and system updatesMedium
  • Build ICP diagnostic tool for Terra menteesMedium
What Success Looks Like
  • Terra runs 30 days without COO — nothing breaks
    If this isn't possible yet, it is the most important thing to build toward. Everything else is secondary.
  • Every lead makes decisions without permission
    Leads come to the COO for support, not approval. That distinction is the measure of system health.
  • Succession is filled across every department
    No department is one departure away from a gap. The COO tracks this monthly.
  • CEO spends zero time on operations
    The CEO is freed for vision and partnerships. The COO owns everything else.
  • Department leads are growing as leaders
    Not just executing tasks. Building judgment, training successors, thinking ahead.

Mentorship Program
Lead: Program Director
"The heart of Terra. Everything else exists to support this."
Why this department exists

Department 04 of 10
Workshops
Lead: Workshop Director
"Where founders first experience what Terra feels like. The front door."
Why this department exists
Workshops are Terra's weekly presence in Bali — free events in Seminyak, Canggu, and Ubud that bring founders together, deliver real value, and create the conditions for people to discover the mentorship program. The Workshop Director owns the full experience: speaker pipeline, quality control, journey structure across locations, and the story of why each workshop matters. They work with Marketing to tell that story and with Logistics to execute it on the ground. A great workshop is the best marketing Terra has.

  • 3 workshops per month across 3 locations — no gaps
    Calendar is set. Speakers are confirmed. Venues are booked. Zero last-minute scramble.

    Every workshop ends with a clear, compelling CTA. Funnel data is captured every time.

Department 06 of 10
Volunteers
Lead: Community Lead
"The people who make Terra run. Treated like the leaders they are."
Why this department exists
Terra runs on human energy. Volunteers are not free labor — they are people who choose to give their time because Terra means something to them. The Community Lead's job is to keep that choice meaningful: matching people to roles that fit, recognising contribution, catching burnout before it becomes departure, and building a clear pathway from volunteer to department lead. When volunteers grow, they stay. The succession pipeline for every department runs through this one.
Cross-Department Dependencies
Feeds trained volunteers into every department · Works with all dept leads on placement and succession · Works with Compliance on burnout signals · Works with COO on promotion to lead roles · Owns the succession pipeline that protects all 8 departments from single-person dependency.

  • Maintain succession plan — 2 candidates per lead roleHigh
  • Promote volunteers to lead roles with COO approvalHigh
  • Manage respectful volunteer offboardingMedium
  • Document volunteer experiences and learningsMedium
What Success Looks Like
  • Retention above 60% cycle-on-cycle
    High turnover means the experience isn't right — not that volunteers aren't committed enough.
  • Every department has 2 succession candidates
