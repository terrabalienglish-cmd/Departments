Operations

Compliance is Terra's memory. Every person who attends an event, enrolls in a course, or enters the mentorship program exists as a record in the Compliance system. Section 01

The Compliance Mandate

Compliance has one purpose: to ensure that every action, commitment, and person in the Terra ecosystem is accurately recorded and that the records are accessible to those who need them when they need them.

Why This Department Exists
Terra runs on relationships and programs — workshops, courses, mentorship cohorts. Every one of these generates data: who attended, who enrolled, who completed, who converted from one program to the next. Without a system that captures and maintains this data with precision, Terra cannot measure what is working, cannot identify who needs follow-up, cannot track financial commitments, and cannot make strategic decisions from evidence. Compliance is the difference between an organisation that knows what it is doing and one that believes it does.
Compliance Owns
  • The People Database
    Master record of every person who has interacted with Terra — attendee, student, mentee, mentor, volunteer, sponsor.
  • Attendance records
    Every event, every session, every course — who was there and who was not.
  • Completion and graduation records
    Who completed School courses. Who graduated from mentorship. Alumni status.
  • Fines and financial commitments tracker
    Mentor fines, payment status for School, sponsorship payment tracking — not accounting, but the record layer.
  • Reporting
    Weekly and monthly data reports to COO, CEO, and department leads.
Compliance Does Not Own
  • Decisions based on the data
    Compliance surfaces the data. Department leads and COO make decisions from it. Compliance Lead does not tell departments what to do — they show them what is true.
  • Financial accounting
    Compliance tracks payment status and commitments. Actual financial management sits with COO.
  • Relationship follow-up
    Compliance flags who needs a follow-up call. The relevant department lead makes the call.
  • Data from outside Terra's programs
    Compliance records what happens inside Terra's programs. External community data is Marketing's domain.
Section 02

Data Flow Architecture

Data enters Compliance from five sources. Each has a defined trigger, a defined format, and a defined deadline. Compliance Lead is responsible for ensuring all upstream sources deliver on time — and for following up when they don't.

Workshop Events
Each workshop
›
Compliance Crew captures every attendee at registration desk. Name, contact, how they heard about Terra, first-time or returning. Uploaded to Master Attendance Sheet → Workshops tab within 2 hours of event end.
SLA: 2h post-event · Owner: Compliance Crew
School Sessions
Each session
›
Compliance Crew or School Support takes attendance within 10 minutes of session start. Submitted to master sheet within 2 hours. School Director is view-only on this data but does not own it.
SLA: 2h post-session · Owner: Compliance Crew / School Support
Mentorship Sessions
Weekly
›
Program Director or mentor logs session completion in the mentorship tracker (Notion). Compliance Lead reconciles weekly against the schedule — flags any session not marked complete by Wednesday each week for the previous week.
SLA: Compliance Lead reconciles every Wednesday
School Enrolment
Per course
›
School Director sends confirmed enrolment list to Compliance Lead within 48h of enrolment closing. Compliance Lead creates student records in People Database and links to the course. Payment status recorded from School Director confirmation.
SLA: 48h after enrolment closes · Owner: School Director sends → Compliance Lead records
Mentorship Applications
Per cohort
›
Program Director sends confirmed cohort list to Compliance Lead before cohort kick-off. Compliance Lead creates or updates mentee and mentor records in People Database. Cohort record created. Session schedule logged.
SLA: Before cohort kick-off · Owner: Program Director sends → Compliance Lead records
The Data Integrity Principle
Every piece of data Compliance holds must be accurate to the day it was entered. Compliance Lead does not estimate, does not round, does not fill gaps with guesses. If a data point is missing, it is flagged as missing — not approximated. A system with known gaps is more trustworthy than a system with hidden approximations. Department leads make better decisions when they know exactly where the data ends.
Section 03

Attendance Tracking

Attendance is the most frequently captured data in Terra. Every workshop, every School session, every mentorship meeting generates an attendance record. The standard is simple: every person who enters a Terra event is counted and identified. No one passes through uncaptured.

Process — Event Attendance Capture
01
Compliance Crew positioned at registration desk before doors open
Compliance Crew arrives with Logistics (T-60min). Registration desk is set up by Logistics — Compliance Crew operates it. The registration method for each event is specified in the Logistics brief: QR code Google Form, paper list, or both. Both methods must be available at every event as backup. QR code is tested before doors open.
Trigger: T-60min before event
Compliance CrewGoogle Form QR
02
Capture every attendee — no exceptions, no shortcuts
Every person who enters is captured: name, WhatsApp number, email, first-time at Terra or returning, how they heard about the event (for first-timers). If someone refuses to give their details, they are noted as "uncaptured attendee" with a tally. No one enters without at least being acknowledged at the registration desk. Community Host supports for anyone who slips through during networking.
Trigger: Doors open
Compliance CrewGoogle Form
03
Late arrivals captured during networking / post-talk
Compliance Crew stays alert to anyone who arrived after the talk started and did not register. During the networking portion, Compliance Crew or Community Host identifies and captures them. Final headcount before event close confirmed against registration count — if the gap is more than 2, Compliance Crew does a sweep of the room for uncaptured attendees.
Trigger: Talk ends, networking begins
Compliance Crew + Community HostGoogle Form or paper
04
Upload data to master sheet within 2 hours of event end
If data was captured via Google Form, it is already in the sheet — Compliance Crew confirms the form responses populated correctly and adds any paper-captured entries manually. Final count confirmed. Any "uncaptured" tally noted. Data upload confirmed to Logistics Lead by WhatsApp message: "[Event name] — [number] attendees captured and logged." If paper list was used, all entries must be typed into the sheet within 2 hours.
Trigger: Event ends
Compliance Crew → Compliance LeadMaster Attendance Sheet
05
Compliance Lead reconciles and updates People Database
Within 24 hours of each event, Compliance Lead reviews the uploaded attendance data. New names are added to the People Database. Returning attendees have their "events attended" count updated. First-time attendees from workshop referral sources are flagged for Marketing — useful for tracking which channels are converting. Any data quality issues (duplicate entries, missing contact info) are resolved immediately.
Trigger: Within 24h of upload confirmation
Compliance LeadPeople Database
School Session Attendance — Specific Protocol

School attendance is subject to the 80% completion threshold — missing 2+ sessions risks a student's graduation eligibility. This makes School attendance data more consequential than workshop attendance. The School Director relies on Compliance data to identify at-risk students.

Capture Standard
  • Taken within first 10 minutes
    Every School session. Marked: Present / Absent / Late (arrived after 10min mark).
  • Uploaded within 2 hours
    To the School Sessions tab in the master sheet. School Director view only.
  • Running completion rate formula
    Each student's running attendance % auto-calculated. At-risk threshold: below 80%.
Flagging Protocol
  • Flag triggered at 2 absences
    Compliance Lead flags the student in Notion and notifies School Director same day.
  • School Director action
    School Director contacts student privately. Compliance does not contact the student.
  • Graduation eligibility list
    Compliance Lead produces this list for School Director before final session: eligible (≥80%) / borderline / ineligible.
Section 04

The People Database

The single most important asset Compliance maintains. Every person who has interacted with Terra — as an attendee, student, mentee, mentor, volunteer, or sponsor — has one record in the People Database. This record follows them as they move through the Terra ecosystem.

One Person, One Record — Always
A founder who attends a workshop, then enrolls in School, then enters mentorship is one person with one record — not three separate entries across three departments. The People Database is the thread that connects the whole Terra journey for each individual. Every department works from their own view of this data, but the record is owned and maintained by Compliance. This is what makes funnel tracking possible.

Section 05

Fines & Financial Commitments

Compliance tracks financial commitments that arise from Terra's programs — mentor session fines, School payment status, sponsorship commitment tracking. Compliance does not collect money and does not chase payments directly. It maintains the record and surfaces the status to COO.

What Compliance Tracks
  • Mentor session fines
    Mentors who cancel or miss sessions without the required notice period are subject to a fine (amount defined in the Mentorship agreement). Compliance logs the fine when triggered by Program Director. Status: outstanding / paid / waived. COO resolves.
  • School payment status
    Every enrolled student: paid / outstanding / payment plan / waived. Updated by Compliance Lead when School Director confirms payment. Outstanding payments flagged to COO weekly if unresolved for 7+ days after enrolment.
  • Sponsorship commitments
    Sponsorship Lead logs the agreed amount and schedule. Compliance tracks whether payment has been received and confirmed by COO. Compliance does not chase sponsors — Sponsorship Lead does. Compliance surfaces the status.
How Fines Are Triggered
  • Mentor no-show or late cancellation
    Program Director identifies the breach, confirms it against the mentorship agreement, and notifies Compliance Lead in writing (WhatsApp or Notion comment). Compliance Lead creates the fine record. Program Director notifies the mentor. COO approves any waiver.
  • School non-payment
    Compliance Lead flags to COO if a student's payment is outstanding 7 days after their enrolment confirmation date. School Director is copied. Action is COO's decision.
  • Waiver process
    Only COO can approve a waiver of any fine or committed payment. Compliance records the waiver decision and the reason. Waivers are never assumed — they are explicitly confirmed in writing.

Section 06

Reporting Cadence

Three reports. Weekly, monthly, and quarterly. Each serves a different decision-making need. The weekly report keeps operations honest. The monthly report drives strategy. The quarterly report reveals the long arc.

Every Monday
Weekly Activity Report
→ COO + all department leads
  • ▸Events held last week (name, date, attendance)
  • ▸School sessions held (which course, attendance rate per session)
  • ▸At-risk School students flagged (below 80% threshold)
  • ▸Mentorship sessions completed vs scheduled
  • ▸Outstanding fines or payments (count and total value)
  • ▸Any data gaps from last week (missing uploads, incomplete records)
Last Day of Month
Monthly Funnel Report
→ COO + CEO + all department leads
  • ▸Total new people entered Terra ecosystem this month
  • ▸Workshop attendance: total, avg per event, returning vs new
  • ▸Workshop → School conversion rate this month
  • ▸School → Mentorship conversion rate this month
  • ▸School completion rates by course
  • ▸Mentorship cohort health (sessions completed, any mentor issues)
  • ▸Referral source breakdown (Instagram / WhatsApp / friend / other)
  • ▸Outstanding payments summary
  • ▸One compliance observation (pattern Compliance Lead has noticed)
End of Quarter
Quarterly Trends Report
→ COO + CEO (strategic review)
  • ▸Total community size (all active Terra contacts)
  • ▸Quarter-on-quarter growth in new entries
  • ▸Funnel conversion rates: workshop → school → mentorship (vs prior quarter)
  • ▸Alumni count growth
  • ▸Mentor fine and payment resolution rate
  • ▸Data quality audit findings
  • ▸Compliance Lead's systemic observations — patterns across the quarter that warrant strategic attention
The Compliance Observation
Every monthly report ends with one observation from the Compliance Lead — a pattern in the data that a department lead or COO may not have noticed. This is the department's highest-value contribution beyond data accuracy. Examples: "Workshop attendees who hear about Terra through Instagram register at a 40% lower rate than those referred by a friend." Or: "School students who attended 3+ workshops before enrolling have a 20% higher completion rate." The data tells a story. Compliance Lead's job is to read it.
Process — Monthly Funnel Report Build
01
Pull all data from master sheets — last day of month
Compliance Lead exports or summarises data from: Master Attendance Sheet (workshop + school tabs), People Database (new entries, alumni count), Mentorship session log, Fines & Commitments tracker. All data from the calendar month. Takes 30–45 minutes for an experienced Compliance Lead with a well-maintained system.
Trigger: Last day of month
Compliance LeadMaster Sheets
02
Calculate funnel conversion rates
Three key conversion calculations: (1) Of workshop attendees this month, how many enrolled in School this month or in the following month? (2) Of School graduates, how many applied for mentorship? (3) Overall: what percentage of new Terra contacts have progressed beyond a single workshop? These numbers are the health metrics of Terra's entire program model.
Trigger: Data pulled
Compliance LeadGoogle Sheets formulas
03
Write the Compliance Observation
One to three sentences. A genuine pattern — not a commentary on whether things are going well. The observation is most useful when it is specific and surprising: something the data reveals that a department lead could not have seen without this aggregated view. Never generic ("attendance was lower this month" is not an observation — it is a data point).
Trigger: Calculations complete
Compliance LeadNotion report page
04
Publish to Notion and notify recipients
Monthly report published to Notion (Compliance Reports section). COO, CEO, and all department leads notified via WhatsApp: "Monthly Funnel Report for [Month] is live in Notion — [one-line summary of the most important number]." Report is not sent as an attachment — it lives in Notion. If a department lead has a question, they respond in Notion or ask Compliance Lead directly.
Trigger: Report complete (last day of month)

Section 09

Operating Rules

Non-Negotiable Operating Rules — Compliance Department
01Attendance data is uploaded within 2 hours of every event ending. Not "tonight" — within 2 hours. If the Compliance Crew member cannot complete the upload within 2 hours, they contact Compliance Lead immediately. The Compliance Lead completes it themselves. The 2-hour rule is what makes the weekly report reliable.
02One person, one record. The People Database never has duplicate entries. If a duplicate is discovered, Compliance Lead merges them within 24 hours and documents what created the duplicate. Duplicates corrupt conversion rate calculations — they are not a minor error.
03The Compliance Lead never estimates or approximates data. If the data is missing, it is recorded as missing. A known gap is more honest and more useful than an approximation. Department leads can work with a known gap. They cannot work with data they believe is accurate but is not.
04The weekly report is published every Monday by 10am. Not "early in the week." Monday 10am. Department leads plan their week from this report. Consistency is what makes it useful.
05Compliance Lead does not make decisions based on the data — they surface it. If a School student is at risk, Compliance flags it to School Director. School Director acts. If a fine is outstanding, Compliance flags it to COO. COO acts. Compliance's authority is in the accuracy of the data, not in the decisions it drives.
06No waiver of any fine or financial commitment is valid without COO written approval. Compliance Lead never approves a waiver independently. The Waiver Log is permanent — every waiver recorded forever, with the reason.
07The monthly Compliance Observation is never skipped. It is the highest-value contribution the department makes beyond data accuracy. Compliance Lead is required to produce one — even if the observation is "this month's data showed no unusual patterns — prior month's pattern of [X] was not repeated."
08Compliance Crew have access only to what they need to do their job: the registration form and the upload sheet. They do not have access to the People Database, the Fines tracker, or the reporting system. Access is commensurate with function.
09The master Google Sheet is never shared outside of the defined access list. Not forwarded, not screenshot-shared in WhatsApp, not shared "temporarily" with anyone. Data privacy is a trust issue — Terra's founders shared their contact information to attend an event, not to be shared.
10Compliance Lead conducts a data quality audit each quarter — reviewing the People Database for: duplicate records, incomplete records, outdated status fields, and formula errors in the Monthly Summary tab. Findings are documented and resolved within 2 weeks of the audit.
11The quarterly Trends Report is presented to COO and CEO as a narrative — not just a spreadsheet export. Compliance Lead writes 3–5 sentences of interpretation for the most significant patterns before submitting. Numbers without context do not drive decisions.
12Compliance Lead trains a deputy who can produce the weekly report, the monthly funnel report, and maintain the People Database independently. The institutional memory of Terra's data cannot live in one person's head.
"Data is only as valuable as the decisions it makes possible."
