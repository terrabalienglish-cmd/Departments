Marketing
Operations

Marketing's job at Terra is not to grow a following. It is to move the right founder from stranger to workshop attendee to School student to mentorship applicant. Every piece of content, every post, every message should serve that journey. This manual defines how Marketing is briefed, how content is produced and approved, how channels are managed, and how performance is measured.

Section 01

Marketing Strategy

Terra is not trying to build a large audience. It is trying to build a precise one. Every strategy decision flows from that distinction.

The One Job
Marketing's singular job is to move the right founder one step further along the Terra journey. A founder who has never heard of Terra should leave their first encounter wanting to attend a workshop. A founder who attended a workshop should leave feeling like School or mentorship is the natural next step. Marketing does not broadcast — it narrows. Every post, every caption, every story should be written to one specific person at one specific stage of their journey.
What Marketing Is and Is Not
Marketing IS
  • Funnel activation
    Every piece of content has a clear next step for the reader — attend, apply, register, respond.
  • Community storytelling
    Founder stories, mentor stories, transformation moments. Real people, real results.
  • Event promotion with story
    Not "Workshop this Friday" but "The reason we're talking about revenue models this Friday."
  • Trust building over time
    Consistent voice, consistent values, consistent quality. Terra should look and sound the same every time.
Marketing IS NOT
  • Follower chasing
    10,000 followers who are not founders in Bali are worth less than 500 who are. Volume is a vanity metric at this stage.
  • Trend-led content
    Terra does not post trending audio reels or participate in content trends that have nothing to do with founders. Consistency of identity beats viral moments.
  • Announcement broadcasting
    "Join our workshop on Friday at 6pm" is not a post. It's a calendar entry. Content tells a story. Announcements are the last line, not the whole post.
  • Reactive posting
    Marketing does not post when departments ask. It posts on a planned calendar, integrating event briefs. Reactive posting produces inconsistent content.
Section 02

Content Pillars

Four pillars govern all content Terra produces. Every post, reel, story, and WhatsApp message belongs to one of these four categories. If a piece of content can't be clearly assigned to a pillar, it shouldn't be made.

Build in Public
35% of content
Founder stories. Lessons from the mentorship program. What someone learned at a workshop. Real progress, real struggle, real insight. Names and permission always obtained first.
Examples: "This founder came to Terra 3 months ago with no idea how to price her offer. Here's where she is now." / Workshop recap with one key insight quoted from the speaker.
Ideas Worth Sitting With
25% of content
Founder thinking, business philosophy, contrarian takes on common advice. Not motivational quotes — genuine, specific, opinionated ideas that the Terra ICP would stop scrolling for.
Examples: "The problem with 'find your niche' advice is that it assumes you know who you are before you start. Most founders don't." / A 3-part carousel on the real reason founders plateau at $5k/month.
Terra Moments
25% of content
Behind the scenes. Workshop energy. The room at a kick-off event. Volunteer faces. The Bali context that makes Terra what it is. Content that makes someone feel what it's like to be there.
Examples: Event day B-roll reel. A photo of 30 founders in a room listening to a speaker with the caption "This is what Thursday looks like." The moment after graduation.
The Invitation
15% of content
Direct CTAs. Workshop announcements. Course launches. Mentorship applications. These are the conversion posts. They must be story-led first, CTA second. Never just a date and a link.
Examples: "If your business has been stuck at the same revenue for the last 4 months, Thursday's workshop is specifically for you. Here's why." Registration link in bio.
The Pillar Mix Discipline
The 35/25/25/15 split is a guide, not a rule to track obsessively. What it prevents is the most common failure mode: an account that posts nothing but event announcements. When The Invitation is 60% of content, the audience learns to scroll past Terra because they know every post is asking something of them. The other three pillars are what earn the right to make the ask.
Section 03

Monthly Content Calendar

The content calendar is built and locked by the 25th of each month for the following month. It integrates all department event briefs, organic content, and pillar distribution. Marketing does not post off the calendar. If it's not on the calendar, it doesn't go out — except for genuine real-time moments at events.

Process — Monthly Calendar Build
01
Collect all department event briefs by 20th of month
Workshops, School, and Mentorship send their event briefs by the 20th. If a brief is not received by the 20th, Marketing Lead follows up once — then proceeds without it. Departments that miss the brief window do not get promotional content that month. This rule exists to protect Marketing's planning process. Exceptions must be approved by COO.
Trigger: 20th of each month
Marketing Lead collectsNotion brief inbox
02
Map events onto the calendar skeleton
Marketing Lead places all confirmed events on the calendar. Each event gets a promotional arc: awareness post (T-14), reminder post (T-7), final push (T-3), day-before WhatsApp (T-1), post-event recap (T+24h). Every promotional arc is mapped before organic content is added around it.
Trigger: All briefs received or 21st, whichever comes first
Marketing LeadNotion calendar
03
Fill organic content slots with pillar-balanced posts
After promotional content is mapped, Marketing Lead fills remaining posting slots with organic content from the four pillars. Checks the running pillar mix — if this month is heavy on Invitation content due to events, ensure Build in Public and Ideas Worth Sitting With are given at least 2 slots each. Organic content ideas are drawn from the Content Ideas backlog in Notion.
Trigger: Event arcs mapped
Marketing LeadNotion content backlog
04
Assign content formats and production tasks
Each calendar slot gets a format assigned: reel, carousel, single image, story, or written post. Production tasks are created in Notion — one task per piece of content, with: post date, format, pillar, caption draft, assets needed, asset source (Content Crew capture or stock or design), and department lead who approves.
Trigger: Calendar skeleton complete
Marketing LeadNotion production tasks
05
Submit calendar to COO for review by 25th
Complete calendar shared with COO in Notion. COO reviews for: pillar balance, alignment with Terra's quarterly priorities, any messaging that needs adjusting. COO has 48 hours to respond. If no response by the 27th, the calendar is considered approved and production begins.
Trigger: By 25th of each month
Marketing Lead → COONotion approval
06
Begin production: captions drafted, assets briefed to Content Crew
Once approved, Marketing Lead writes all captions for the month and briefs Content Crew on any capture needed at upcoming events. Content Crew gets a written brief — format, moment to capture, deadline for delivery. Every caption is reviewed against the Terra voice guide before it goes to the relevant department lead for final approval.
Trigger: Calendar approved

Section 04

Brief Intake Process

Every piece of promotional content starts with a brief from the requesting department. Marketing does not create content based on a WhatsApp message or a verbal request. A brief is submitted. A brief is reviewed. Then production begins.

Department sends
Complete Brief
Event name, date, venue, story angle, CTA, ICP, assets needed, approval contact, deadline.
→ Via Notion template
›
Marketing Lead
Brief Review (24h)
Checks brief is complete. Confirms timeline is feasible. Flags anything missing. Accepts or returns with questions.
SLA: 24h acknowledgement
›
Marketing Lead
Deliver Assets (5 days)
First draft of all requested assets submitted to department lead for approval. Captions, graphics, reel script if applicable.
SLA: 5 business days from complete brief
What Makes a Brief Complete
A brief is not complete if any of these are missing: the story angle (not just the logistics), the exact CTA wording, who the content is for (ICP description), the approval contact and their turnaround time commitment, and the publish deadline. An incomplete brief is returned to the sender with a note on what's missing. Marketing does not produce content from an incomplete brief — incomplete briefs produce content that misses the point and requires multiple revision rounds.

Section 05

Channel Management

Each channel has a specific purpose, a specific audience, and a specific tone. Content is not cross-posted carelessly — it is adapted. What works on Instagram does not work in a WhatsApp community and should not be copy-pasted.

ChannelPurposeAudienceTonePosting CadenceWhat Does Not Belong
Instagram Feed
 Discovery and trust-building. New founders find Terra here. Existing community stays connected. Founders in Bali and SE Asia. Digital nomads. Anyone who might attend a workshop. Warm, direct, opinionated. Sounds like a founder, not a brand. 3–4 posts per week. Quality ceiling: nothing published below Terra's visual standard. Generic motivational quotes. Trend-led audio that has no connection to Terra's identity. Blurry or poorly lit photos.
Instagram Reels
 Reach and community feel. Event energy, founder stories, behind-the-scenes. Broader reach than feed — first touch for many new followers. Energetic but not frantic. Bali context is visible. People are real. 1–2 reels per week. Every major event produces at least one reel within 24h. Reels made purely for algorithm without editorial intent. Overproduced content that loses authenticity.
Instagram Stories
 Daily presence. Event reminders. Behind-the-scenes. Community warmth. Existing followers. People already interested in Terra. Most casual of all channels. Can be spontaneous — but never sloppy. Daily when events are active. 3–4x per week minimum otherwise. Long blocks of text. Unreadable graphics. Reposts of irrelevant content.
WhatsApp Community
 Existing community announcements. Event reminders. Recap links. Community warmth. People who have already attended Terra events or opted in. The warm audience. Personal, direct. Reads like a message from a person, not a brand. First-person voice. Structured per event arc (T-7, T-1, T+24h recap). Maximum 3 WhatsApp messages per week — respect the inbox. Promotional tone. Irrelevant content. Messages that belong on Instagram instead. Anything posted more than 3x per week outside of event weeks.
Email (registrants)
 Post-event follow-up to registered attendees. CTA-driven. People who registered for a specific event. Already interested. Personal, specific to what they attended. Not a newsletter blast. Within 24h post-event. Triggered by event, not scheduled. Generic content. Mass newsletter format. Anything that doesn't reference the specific event they attended.
The Voice Consistency Rule
Terra's voice is warm, direct, and specific. It sounds like a confident founder who has seen a lot and respects their audience enough to be honest with them. It does not use buzzwords ("leverage your potential," "unlock your impact"), it does not write vague captions ("Big things are coming 👀"), and it does not hedge ("We hope this might be useful for some of you"). Every caption should be rewritable as something a real person would say out loud. If it sounds like a brand, rewrite it until it sounds like a person.
Section 06

Content Approval Flow

Every piece of content produced from a department brief must be approved by the relevant department lead before it is published. Organic content (pillars 1–3) is approved by the Marketing Lead and COO on the monthly calendar. The approval flow below applies to all promotional content.

01
Marketing Lead completes draft assets
Caption drafted, graphic designed or asset selected, reel edited if applicable. All assets are reviewed internally against the voice guide and brand standards before sending for approval. Marketing Lead does not submit rough drafts — they submit their best work.
Trigger: Within 5 business days of complete brief
Marketing LeadNotion task → review status
02
Assets submitted to department lead for approval
Shared via Notion (preferred) or Google Drive link. Department lead receives: the caption, the visual asset(s), the proposed publish date and channel. Clear prompt: "Approve or feedback by [date] — content publishes on [date]." Department lead has 24 hours to respond. If no response in 24h, Marketing Lead sends one follow-up. If still no response after 48h total, content is escalated to COO.
Trigger: Assets ready
Marketing Lead → Dept LeadNotion approval field
03
Department lead reviews for accuracy — not design
The department lead's job in the approval: check that the information is accurate (date, time, venue, CTA), that the story angle is correct, and that the message aligns with what they briefed. They are not approving the design aesthetic or the font choices — that is Marketing's domain. Feedback must be specific: "The date is wrong, it's Thursday not Friday" — not "can we make it feel more exciting?"
Trigger: Assets received by department lead
Department LeadNotion — approved / feedback
04
Revisions (if needed) and final publish
If factual corrections are needed, Marketing Lead amends and resubmits. One revision round for factual errors. If a department lead requests subjective changes that conflict with Marketing's editorial judgment, Marketing Lead flags the disagreement to COO — not to the department lead directly. COO is the tiebreaker. Content is published on the scheduled date once approved.
Trigger: Approval received
Marketing Lead publishesPlatform native / scheduler
05
Published content logged in Notion
Every published post is logged in the Content Database with: publish date, channel, pillar, event link (if applicable), and a note of performance at the 7-day mark (reach, engagement rate, link clicks if applicable). This log feeds the monthly performance review.
Trigger: Post published
Marketing LeadNotion content log
Section 07

Performance Metrics

Four metrics govern Marketing performance. All others are context, not KPIs. The monthly review takes 30 minutes and produces one decision: what changes next month and what stays.

01
Primary KPI
Funnel entries per month. People who registered for an event, applied for School, or applied for mentorship — attributed to Marketing's reach.
02
Engagement Rate
On promotional posts specifically. Target: above platform average for Bali founder content. Below average triggers a content review — not a volume increase.
03
Event Registration Rate
Of people who saw a workshop announcement post, what percentage registered? Tracked via UTM links on all registration URLs. Below 2% triggers a message review.
04
WhatsApp Open Rate
Estimated by response rate and link click-through. If engagement in WhatsApp is declining, the content or frequency is wrong. Check both before changing either.
Process — Monthly Marketing Review
01
Pull 4-metric performance report on last day of month
Marketing Lead pulls data from Instagram Insights, registration sheet (Compliance), and WhatsApp link tracking. Logs into Notion Monthly Review template. Takes 20 minutes maximum — this is not a deep dive, it's a snapshot.
Trigger: Last day of each month
Marketing LeadNotion monthly review
02
Identify the one thing that underperformed most and why
Not a full audit. One clear question: what produced the least result relative to effort this month? Was it the event announcement cadence? A specific pillar of content? The WhatsApp message timing? Name it specifically. Vague reviews produce vague improvements.
Trigger: Data pulled
Marketing LeadNotion review notes
03
Make one change to next month's calendar based on the finding
One change per review cycle. Not a strategy overhaul. If the T-7 reminder post underperformed, test a different format next month. If Build in Public content got strong engagement but only appeared twice, add two more slots next month. Small, specific, measurable changes. Document the change and the hypothesis in Notion so results can be assessed at the next review.
Trigger: Underperformer identified
Marketing LeadNotion calendar (next month)
04
Share summary with COO — one paragraph
Monthly review summary shared with COO in Notion. One paragraph: what the numbers were, what worked, what didn't, what changes next month. COO does not need a report — they need a decision. The summary ends with the one change being made and why. COO responds with any concerns or adjustments within 48h.
Trigger: Review complete
Marketing Lead → COONotion

Section 10

Operating Rules

Non-Negotiable Operating Rules — Marketing Department
01Zero content published without department lead approval on promotional posts, and without Marketing Lead approval on organic posts. Zero exceptions. A post that goes out unapproved — even if it's good — undermines the system. The system is what makes quality consistent.
02Marketing does not accept verbal or WhatsApp briefs. Every brief comes through the Notion template. Incomplete briefs are returned, not started. The brief is the contract between Marketing and the requesting department.
03Department leads have 24 hours to approve content or provide specific factual feedback. If they do not respond within 24 hours, Marketing Lead follows up once. After 48 hours, the item is escalated to COO. Marketing does not wait indefinitely.
04The content calendar is locked by the 25th of each month for the following month. Emergency additions (genuine breaking moments at Terra) are approved by Marketing Lead only — they do not require a full brief cycle. Everything else follows the calendar.
05Follower count is not a KPI. It is never reported as a success metric. The only metrics that matter are funnel entries, engagement rate on promotional content, and event registration rate. Vanity metrics produce vanity strategy.
06Content Crew delivers raw assets to Google Drive within 24 hours of every event. If assets are not received within 24 hours, Marketing Lead contacts the Content Crew directly — not the Volunteer Lead. One escalation, same day.
07The Terra voice is protected. If a caption sounds like a brand, it is rewritten until it sounds like a person. If a department lead requests wording that violates the voice guide, Marketing Lead proposes an alternative. If agreement cannot be reached, COO decides.
08WhatsApp community messages are capped at 3 per week outside of active event weeks. Respect for the inbox is respect for the community. Over-messaging trains people to mute Terra.
09Every piece of promotional content includes a clear, singular CTA. Not two CTAs. Not "follow us, register, and share." One ask per post. Multiple asks dilute all of them.
10The monthly performance review produces one change — not a strategy overhaul. Consistency is more valuable than reactivity. If the same thing underperforms for 3 consecutive months, then a structural review is warranted. One month of data is a data point, not a verdict.
11The Brand & Voice Guide is immutable except through a COO-approved update process. Individual departments cannot request changes to brand standards. Brand consistency over 12 months is worth more than any single event's aesthetic preference.
12Marketing Lead trains a deputy who can run the full monthly calendar cycle — brief intake, production, approval, publish — independently. The department does not stop if the Marketing Lead is unavailable for 2 weeks.
